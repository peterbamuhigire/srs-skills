### 1. `habit-forming-products` (`C:/Users/Peter/.claude/skills/habit-forming-products/SKILL.md`)

Add the following 6 sections:

**A. Habit Discovery (Pre-Design Section)**
Four methods for identifying habit-forming opportunities before designing:
1. Look in the Mirror — solve your own pain (Paul Graham)
2. Nascent Behaviors — watch early adopters doing niche things that foreshadow mass adoption
3. Enabling Technologies — identify infrastructure/platform waves that make Hook cycles faster
4. Interface Changes — major UI revolutions (GUI, mobile, infinite scroll) unlock new habit surfaces

**B. Finite vs. Infinite Variability**
- Finite: predictable payoff schedules eventually bore users (slot machines with known odds)
- Infinite: open-ended social feeds, open-world games, search results — sustains indefinitely
- Design goal: build infinite variability wherever possible

**C. Reciprocation as Investment Mechanism**
- Stanford study: participants did ~2× more work for machines that had been helpful
- Reciprocity operates even with non-human systems
- Implication: deliver genuine value first, then ask for investment

**D. The 4 Persuasion Heuristics (Action-Phase Amplifiers)**
- Scarcity Effect: perceived scarcity raises perceived value (Amazon "14 left in stock")
- Framing Effect: context shapes reward perception (Joshua Bell subway vs. concert hall)
- Anchoring Effect: first number seen anchors all subsequent judgments (discount pricing)
- Social Proof & Authority: majority behavior + expert endorsement increase action likelihood

**E. Workbook Application Framework**
Structured 8-exercise sequence from the Supplemental Workbook — converts the Hook Model into a practical workshop format for product teams.

**F. Habit Testing Benchmarks**
Specific data points from the book:
- `< 5%` habitual users = fundamental product problem
- Twitter tipping point: following `≥ 30` accounts predicted long-term retention
- `79%` of smartphone owners check device within 15 minutes of waking
- `26%` of mobile apps used only once and abandoned (2010)
