# Section 1: Introduction to the Advanced Inventory Module (F-014)

## 1.1 Purpose

This document specifies the Software Requirements Specification (SRS) for the Advanced Inventory module (F-014) of the Maduuka SaaS platform. It is written per IEEE Std 830-1998 and covers the full scope of Phase 3b. The intended audience is the Maduuka development team, quality assurance engineers, and the product owner.

## 1.2 Scope

The Advanced Inventory module extends the Phase 1 Inventory and Stock Management module (F-002) with capabilities required by medium-to-large trading, manufacturing, and import-focused businesses. All Phase 1 inventory functional requirements (FR-INV-001 through FR-INV-018) remain in force. Where a Phase 3b requirement modifies or supersedes a Phase 1 behaviour, the supersession is stated explicitly in the relevant requirement.

F-014 is an opt-in add-on priced at +UGX 25,000 per month above the subscriber's base plan. Tenants who do not subscribe to F-014 continue to operate under F-002 rules only.

The module covers the following capability areas:

- Multi-warehouse management (unlimited warehouses per branch)
- Serial number tracking for individual serialised items
- Batch traceability from purchase to sale with product recall support
- Landed cost allocation across imported shipments
- Bill of materials (BOM) and production order management with yield variance tracking
- Demand forecasting and reorder quantity recommendation
- Compliance audit reporting for high-value and unusual stock movements

## 1.3 Relationship to Phase 1 Inventory (F-002)

F-014 extends F-002; it does not replace it. The following rules govern the relationship:

- All stock movement records created by F-014 operations are immutable, per the F-002 rule in FR-INV-005.
- The FIFO/FEFO valuation methods from F-002 apply to all stock movements generated by F-014, including production completions and landed cost updates.
- Reorder alerts from F-002 (FR-INV-007) continue to fire; F-014 supplements them with demand-forecasting alerts (FR-AINV-063) at the per-product level.
- The stock count workflow from F-002 (FR-INV-014) operates per warehouse; F-014 adds warehouse-level count management without altering the freeze-count-variance-approve sequence.

## 1.4 Definitions

**Batch** -- A uniquely identified lot of product received in a single goods receipt event, characterised by a batch number, manufacturing date, and expiry date. Defined per IEEE Std 610.12-1990 as a bounded collection of items processed together.

**Bill of Materials (BOM)** -- A structured list specifying the raw material SKUs and their required quantities needed to produce one unit of a finished good, together with the theoretical yield percentage.

**Cross-Warehouse Availability** -- The aggregate of available (on-hand minus reserved) stock for a given product across all warehouses within a tenant's account, queried in a single operation.

**Days of Stock Remaining** -- The estimated number of calendar days before current stock is exhausted, calculated as: $DaysRemaining = \frac{CurrentStock}{AverageDailySales_{30d}}$ where $AverageDailySales_{30d}$ is the rolling 30-day average daily unit sales.

**Demand Forecast** -- A forward-looking estimate of stock requirements based on historical velocity and configured lead time and safety stock parameters.

**FEFO** -- First Expired, First Out. An inventory issue sequence that dispatches stock with the earliest expiry date first, defined in F-002 and enforced by FR-INV-011.

**FIFO** -- First In, First Out. An inventory valuation and issue sequence that assigns cost from the earliest receipt first, defined in F-002.

**Finished Good** -- A product produced from raw materials via a production order, resulting in a sellable SKU that is incremented in the inventory upon production completion.

**Landed Cost** -- The total cost of a product after all import-related charges have been allocated, including freight, import duty, insurance, clearing fees, and handling charges, as defined per ASTM E1340 cost accounting conventions.

**Landed Cost Allocation** -- The distribution of total import charges across individual products in a shipment using a configurable allocation method (by value, weight, quantity, or volume).

**Production Order** -- A formal instruction to produce a specified quantity of a finished good, triggering raw material reservation, consumption, and finished-good stock increment upon completion.

**Reorder Quantity Recommendation** -- The computed quantity to order when stock reaches the reorder trigger point, calculated as: $ReorderQty = (AverageDailySales_{30d} \times LeadTimeDays) + SafetyStock - CurrentStock$

**Serial Number** -- A unique alphanumeric identifier assigned to an individual physical unit of a serialised product, enabling full movement history tracking from goods receipt to sale.

**UOM Conversion Matrix** -- A product-level table of all defined selling and purchasing units of measure and their conversion factors relative to the product's base unit.

**Warehouse** -- A named, addressable physical or logical location within a branch where stock is held. Warehouse types are: storage (primary holding), transit (in-transit between warehouses), and returns (returned goods awaiting inspection or disposal).

**Yield** -- The actual usable output from a production run expressed as a percentage of the theoretical BOM output. Yield variance is the difference between actual yield and the BOM-defined theoretical yield.

**Yield Variance** -- The deviation of actual production output from the BOM-defined theoretical yield: $YieldVariance = ActualYield\% - TheoreticalYield\%$. A negative variance indicates raw material loss exceeding the BOM tolerance.
