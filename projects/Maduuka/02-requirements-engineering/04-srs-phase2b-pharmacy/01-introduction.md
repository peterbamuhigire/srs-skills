# Section 1: Introduction to the Pharmacy/Drug Store Module SRS

## 1.1 Purpose

This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) defines the functional and non-functional requirements for the Pharmacy/Drug Store add-on module (F-012) of the Maduuka platform. It is addressed to the development team, quality assurance engineers, NDA Uganda inspectors (read-only access), and the Chwezi Core Systems product owner.

This document governs Phase 2b of the Maduuka build and supplements — but does not replace — the Phase 1 SRS. All Phase 1 requirements remain in force. This SRS introduces requirements specific to pharmaceutical dispensing, prescription management, controlled substance compliance, and pharmacy-specific billing.

## 1.2 Scope

The Pharmacy/Drug Store module is an optional add-on available to Maduuka subscribers at +UGX 30,000/month. It activates upon subscription and integrates directly with the Phase 1 Inventory and Stock Management module (F-002) and the Phase 1 Point of Sale module (F-001).

The module covers:

- Patient profile management including allergy registers and insurance details.
- Prescription intake, attachment, lifecycle tracking, and dispensing linkage.
- A specialised pharmacy Point of Sale (POS) with FEFO batch selection, dispensing unit conversion, allergy alerting, and basic drug interaction warnings.
- A drug reference database with generic names, brand names, drug class, controlled substance classification, and storage requirements.
- Batch and expiry management with near-expiry alerting and cold chain temperature logging.
- A controlled drugs register compliant with NDA Uganda requirements.
- NDA compliance audit log export.
- Insurance billing and claim tracking.
- Refill reminder delivery via SMS and WhatsApp.

This module does not cover:

- Clinical decision support, clinical pharmacovigilance, or any function requiring medical-grade AI certification.
- Electronic prescriptions issued by a health information system (HIS) integration — prescription data is entered manually or via photo/scan.
- IoT-based temperature sensor integration — cold chain logging is manual entry in Phase 2.

## 1.3 Relationship to Phase 1 SRS

Pharmacy products are inventory items. The F-012 module extends the F-002 product record with pharmacy-specific attributes (drug class, controlled classification, dispensing unit, storage requirements) without replacing the core inventory data model. All stock movements generated by pharmacy dispensing follow the immutable stock movement rules defined in BR-004 and the FEFO enforcement rules defined in BR-006.

The pharmacy POS is a specialised mode of the F-001 POS. Session management (BR-007), void and refund, and multi-payment rules defined in the Phase 1 SRS apply unless explicitly superseded in this document.

## 1.5 Regulatory Context

The National Drug Authority (NDA) of Uganda, established under the National Drug Policy and Authority Act (Cap 206), regulates the importation, exportation, manufacture, distribution, sale, and use of drugs in Uganda. The Uganda Pharmacy and Drugs Act Cap 280 governs the practice of pharmacy, the dispensing of prescription-only medicines, and the maintenance of controlled drugs records.

Maduuka F-012 is a pharmacy business management tool. It is not itself a licensed pharmaceutical entity. The pharmacist or pharmacy business operating Maduuka retains full regulatory responsibility for compliance with NDA Uganda requirements. Maduuka provides the data capture, record-keeping, and export tools required to support that compliance.

## 1.6 Disclaimer — Drug Interaction Warnings

*This system provides basic category-level drug interaction warnings as a decision-support aid only. It does not replace clinical pharmacist judgement. Maduuka is not certified as a clinical decision support system.*

This disclaimer is displayed on every drug interaction warning generated by the system (see FR-PHR-034) and reproduced in the onboarding configuration interface.

## 1.7 Definitions

| Term | Definition (IEEE Std 610.12-1990 basis) |
|---|---|
| Generic name | The non-proprietary International Nonproprietary Name (INN) assigned to a drug active ingredient (e.g., Amoxicillin). |
| Brand name | The proprietary trade name under which a manufacturer markets a drug product (e.g., Amoxil). A single generic name may have multiple brand names. |
| FEFO | First Expiry, First Out. A stock rotation method in which the batch with the earliest expiry date is selected for dispensing before batches with later expiry dates, regardless of receipt date. |
| Controlled substance | A drug or chemical classified under the Uganda Pharmacy and Drugs Act Cap 280 as subject to special controls due to dependency potential or abuse risk. Classified S1–S4 per the Uganda drug schedule. |
| Cold chain | The temperature-controlled supply and storage environment required for certain pharmaceutical products. Standard refrigerated cold chain for drugs is 2–8°C. |
| Dispensing label | A patient-specific label affixed to a dispensed drug package, containing at minimum: patient name, drug name, dosage, administration instructions, pharmacist name, dispensing date, batch number, and expiry date. |
| Prescription-linked dispensing | The practice of recording a sale of a prescription-only medicine against a verified, recorded prescription before dispensing is permitted, in compliance with BR-013. |
| Formulary | The approved list of drugs stocked and dispensed by a pharmacy, referencing NDA Uganda drug codes and classifications. |
| NDA | National Drug Authority of Uganda — the statutory body responsible for regulating drugs and clinical devices. |
| PII | Personally Identifiable Information — any data that can directly or indirectly identify an individual patient, as defined under the Uganda Data Protection and Privacy Act 2019. |
| NIN | National Identification Number — the unique identifier issued by NIRA Uganda to every registered Ugandan citizen or resident. |
