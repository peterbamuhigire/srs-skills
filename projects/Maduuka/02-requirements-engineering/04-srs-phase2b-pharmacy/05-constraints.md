# Section 5: Constraints on the Pharmacy/Drug Store Module

Constraints are conditions that restrict system design choices without being requirements for system behaviour. They are not optional. Each constraint is categorised as regulatory, data, technical, or operational.

---

## 5.1 Regulatory Constraints

**CON-PHR-001 — Not a Clinical Decision Support System (Regulatory)**

Maduuka F-012 is not certified as a Clinical Decision Support System (CDSS) under any applicable regulatory framework, including but not limited to FDA 21 CFR Part 820, CE Medical Device Regulation (EU) 2017/745, or NDA Uganda device classification. The system shall not make, suggest, or imply clinical treatment decisions. Drug interaction warnings are limited to category-level informational alerts (see FR-PHR-035). This is a hard legal constraint: no future feature in F-012 shall cross the boundary into clinical decision support without a separate regulatory certification process.

The mandatory disclaimer stated in Section 1.6 must appear on every drug interaction warning generated by the system and must be reproduced verbatim. It may not be shortened, paraphrased, or made dismissible by configuration.

**CON-PHR-002 — Controlled Drugs Register: Append-Only (Regulatory)**

The controlled drugs register is a legal document under the Uganda Pharmacy and Drugs Act Cap 280 and NDA Uganda regulations. No user role — including platform admin — shall be permitted to edit or delete a register entry via any application interface. This constraint is non-negotiable regardless of business configuration. Corrections require an explicit reversal entry per FR-PHR-073.

**CON-PHR-003 — NDA Formulary Dependency (Regulatory/Data)**

The drug reference database pre-loaded at pharmacy activation depends on the NDA Uganda approved formulary and drug codes. This data has not yet been obtained. [CONTEXT-GAP: GAP-003 — NDA Uganda approved drug codes and formulary list not yet obtained. The pharmacy module cannot be launched with a production-complete drug reference database until GAP-003 is resolved by the product owner.] Development and testing shall proceed with a representative synthetic drug dataset. The production dataset requires NDA Uganda approval before go-live.

**CON-PHR-004 — Controlled Drugs Register Format Pending (Regulatory/Data)**

The exact output format, required fields, and data retention period for the controlled drugs register export are not yet confirmed by NDA Uganda. [CONTEXT-GAP: GAP-009 — Exact NDA Uganda register format and retention period not confirmed. The export format specified in FR-PHR-069 and FR-PHR-070 is provisional.] The register data model shall be designed to accommodate field additions without migration complexity. The export template shall be updatable by the platform admin without a code release.

**CON-PHR-005 — Uganda Data Protection and Privacy Act 2019 (Regulatory)**

Patient profiles contain PII subject to the Uganda Data Protection and Privacy Act 2019 (DPPA 2019). The following constraints apply:

- Patient PII shall not be stored outside the tenant's data region without explicit consent.
- NDA Inspector access to patient PII shall be limited to the minimum fields required for controlled drugs register review (patient name and NIN).
- Patient records shall be deletable upon a verified data subject request, except where deletion would remove records required for regulatory compliance (controlled drugs register entries). In that case, the personal identifiers shall be anonymised and the register record retained.

## 5.2 Technical Constraints

**CON-PHR-006 — Cold Chain Logging: Manual Entry Only (Technical)**

Cold chain temperature logging in Phase 2 is manual data entry by pharmacy staff. No IoT temperature sensor integration, Bluetooth sensor protocol, or automated data feed is in scope for Phase 2. The system shall not imply automated monitoring in any UI label or notification. Labels shall read "Manual temperature log" not "Temperature monitoring."

**CON-PHR-007 — Drug Interaction Database Source Undefined (Technical)**

The category-level drug interaction check (FR-PHR-035) depends on a drug-class-to-interaction mapping table. The source of this mapping (proprietary database, open-source dataset such as DrugBank Open Data, or custom-curated table) has not been determined. [CONTEXT-GAP: drug interaction database source not defined — FR-PHR-034 and FR-PHR-035 cannot be fully implemented until this source is identified, licensed, and loaded.] The interaction check feature shall be flagged as "pending data source" in the Phase 2 build plan.

**CON-PHR-008 — iOS Thermal Printing Compatibility (Technical)**

Label printing on iOS (dispensing label PDF output to a label printer) is subject to the resolution of GAP-004 (iOS thermal printing compatibility). If GAP-004 is not resolved before Phase 2 iOS build, label printing on iOS shall fall back to A4 PDF generation only. The 57 mm × 32 mm label print format (FR-PHR-039) shall be delivered on iOS only after GAP-004 is confirmed.

**CON-PHR-009 — WhatsApp Refill Reminders: Africa's Talking Dependency (Technical)**

WhatsApp-based refill reminders (FR-PHR-086) depend on Africa's Talking WhatsApp Business API access, which is pending confirmation (GAP-006). SMS refill reminders (FR-PHR-085) via Africa's Talking standard SMS API are not subject to this constraint and shall be delivered in Phase 2 regardless of GAP-006 status.

## 5.3 Operational Constraints

**CON-PHR-010 — Pharmacist Role Required for Controlled Substance Dispensing**

A tenant must have at least 1 active user assigned the Pharmacist role at all times that controlled substances are in stock. The system shall not permit controlled substance dispensing if no Pharmacist role user is logged in, regardless of business configuration.

**CON-PHR-011 — Platform Admin Manages Drug Reference Database**

Tenants do not have self-service rights to add, edit, or delete drug records in the drug reference database. This constraint ensures data integrity across all tenants and prevents unauthorised drug classifications. Tenants may flag missing drugs via an in-app request mechanism; additions are made by the platform admin after validation.
