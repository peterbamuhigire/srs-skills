# Customers, Suppliers, and Finance

## Adding a Customer and Setting a Credit Limit

1. Go to **More** → **Customers**.
2. Tap the **+** button.
3. Fill in the customer details:
   - **Name** (required)
   - **Phone Number** (required — used for WhatsApp receipts and SMS)
   - **Email** (optional)
   - **Customer Group** — select **Retail**, **Wholesale**, **VIP**, or **Staff**. The group determines which price tier this customer sees.
4. To allow the customer to buy on credit, turn on **Credit Account**.
5. Enter the **Credit Limit** — the maximum amount this customer can owe at one time (for example, `200,000`).
6. Tap **Save Customer**.

The customer is now available in the POS when processing a credit sale.

## Recording a Payment from a Credit Customer

When a credit customer comes to pay what they owe:

1. Go to **More** → **Customers**.
2. Search for and tap the customer's name.
3. Tap **Collect Payment**.
4. Enter the **Amount Paid**.
5. Select the payment method: **Cash**, **MTN MoMo**, or **Airtel Money**.
6. Tap **Confirm Payment**.

The customer's outstanding balance reduces immediately. A payment receipt can be printed or sent via WhatsApp.

## Creating a Purchase Order to a Supplier

A Purchase Order (PO) is a document you send to a supplier listing the items you want to buy.

1. Go to **More** → **Suppliers**.
2. Tap the supplier you want to order from.
3. Tap **New Purchase Order**.
4. Tap **Add Item** for each product you are ordering. Enter the product, quantity, and agreed unit price.
5. Review the order total.
6. Tap **Save PO** to save as a draft, or tap **Send PO** to generate a PDF and share it with the supplier via WhatsApp or email.

## Receiving Goods Against a Purchase Order

When the supplier delivers the goods you ordered:

1. Go to **Stock** → **Receive Stock** → **From Purchase Order**.
2. Select the PO from the list.
3. Maduuka shows the expected items and quantities.
4. Enter the **actual quantity received** for each item. If some items are short-delivered, enter the lower quantity — the PO stays open for the remaining items.
5. Enter batch numbers and expiry dates if applicable.
6. Tap **Confirm Receipt**.

Stock levels update and a supplier invoice record is created for the amount owed.

## Recording an Expense with a Receipt Photo

1. Go to **More** → **Expenses**.
2. Tap **New Expense**.
3. Fill in the details:
   - **Date** — tap to select the date of the expense
   - **Category** — for example, `Rent`, `Electricity`, `Transport`, `Staff Lunch`
   - **Amount**
   - **Payment Method** — select how the expense was paid (cash, MoMo, etc.)
   - **Description** — a short note about what this expense was for
4. To attach a receipt photo, tap **Add Receipt Photo**. Take a photo of the paper receipt. Maduuka can read the amount and vendor automatically using the phone camera.
5. Tap **Save Expense**.

Expenses above the threshold set by the business owner require manager approval before they are posted to the accounts.

## Viewing Account Balances

1. Go to **More** → **Finance** → **Accounts**.
2. The accounts screen shows all your payment accounts:
   - **Cash Till** — current cash balance
   - **MTN MoMo Business** — MoMo account balance
   - **Airtel Money Business** — Airtel account balance
   - **Bank Account(s)** — if configured
3. Tap any account to see the full transaction history for that account — every inflow and outflow, with dates and descriptions.

## How to Read the Debtors Ageing Report

The *debtors ageing report* shows you which customers owe you money and for how long they have owed it.

1. Go to **More** → **Reports** → **Debtors Ageing**.
2. Select the date range and tap **Run Report**.

The report is organised in columns:

| Column | What it means |
|---|---|
| Customer | The customer's name |
| Current | Amount owed for less than 30 days |
| 31–60 days | Amount owed for 31 to 60 days |
| 61–90 days | Amount owed for 61 to 90 days |
| Over 90 days | Amount owed for more than 90 days (overdue) |
| Total | Total amount this customer owes you |

Customers in the **Over 90 days** column need to be contacted urgently — this money has been outstanding for 3 months.

To print or export the report, tap **Export PDF** or **Export CSV** at the top of the report.
