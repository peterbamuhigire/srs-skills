# Business Rules — Medic8

## BR-CLIN: Clinical Rules

- BR-CLIN-001: Triage priority levels determine queue position across all departments. Emergency: immediate attention, jumps all queues including lab and radiology. Urgent: seen within 30 minutes of triage. Semi-urgent: seen within 60 minutes of triage. Non-urgent: seen in queue order. The system enforces queue-jumping for emergency patients in every service queue (OPD, lab, radiology, pharmacy).
- BR-CLIN-002: Prescribing authority is role-restricted. Doctors may prescribe all medications on the formulary. Clinical Officers may prescribe within their gazetted scope of practice. Nurses may NOT prescribe; they administer prescribed medications only. Pharmacists may substitute a generic equivalent when the branded drug is unavailable, provided the requesting doctor is notified and the substitution is logged.
- BR-CLIN-003: Critical value escalation follows a timed cascade. Lab results exceeding panic thresholds (e.g., potassium > 6.5 mmol/L, glucose < 2.5 mmol/L) trigger an immediate notification to the requesting doctor. If the notification is not acknowledged within 30 minutes, the system escalates to the ward sister. If not acknowledged within 60 minutes, the system escalates to the Facility Admin. All escalation steps are timestamped in the audit trail.
- BR-CLIN-004: Drug interaction alerts use a four-tier severity classification. Tier 1 (Info): displayed passively in the prescription sidebar, no clinician action required. Tier 2 (Warning): displayed prominently with an amber banner, clinician may proceed without override. Tier 3 (Serious): modal alert blocks the workflow, clinician must provide a documented override reason to proceed. Tier 4 (Fatal): hard stop that cannot be overridden by the prescriber; a pharmacist must intervene and resolve the interaction before the prescription is accepted. All Tier 3 and Tier 4 overrides are logged with clinician ID, timestamp, and reason.
- BR-CLIN-005: Medication reconciliation is mandatory at every transition of care: OPD to IPD admission, ward-to-ward transfer, IPD to discharge, and facility-to-facility referral. The system generates a reconciliation form listing all active medications, their dosages, frequencies, and routes. The receiving clinician must review and confirm the reconciliation before the transition is finalised.
- BR-CLIN-006: Paediatric dosing safeguards apply to all patients under 12 years. All prescriptions must use weight-based dosing (mg/kg). The system calculates the dose from the patient's recorded weight, applies the adult ceiling dose cap, and flags any calculated dose that exceeds 10x the expected dose as a potential decimal error. Patient weight must be recorded within 24 hours of prescribing; the system blocks prescription submission if no weight is on file within this window.
- BR-CLIN-007: Early Warning Score calculation uses the NEWS2 (National Early Warning Score 2) framework. Vital signs entered by nursing staff trigger automatic NEWS2 score computation. Score 0-4: routine monitoring at standard intervals. Score 5-6: increase monitoring frequency, notify the responsible doctor. Score 7+: immediate clinical review required, consider ICU transfer. Population-adjusted thresholds for Sub-Saharan Africa baselines are applied where clinically validated.
- BR-CLIN-008: Five Rights enforcement is embedded in the Computerised Physician Order Entry (CPOE) system. Every prescription submission validates: right patient (patient ID confirmed at point of entry), right drug (selected from the facility formulary), right dose (within the therapeutic range for the drug and patient weight), right route (valid administration route for the selected drug), and right time (frequency schedule is clinically appropriate). Failure of any right blocks prescription submission with a specific error message identifying the failed check.
- BR-CLIN-009: Discharge documentation completeness is enforced by the system. Discharge cannot be finalised until all of the following are recorded: diagnosis (ICD-10 coded), discharge summary (completed by the attending doctor), medication reconciliation at discharge (BR-CLIN-005), follow-up plan (documented with date and responsible clinician), and billing settlement (balance paid or credit arrangement approved by Facility Admin).

## BR-FIN: Financial Rules

- BR-FIN-001: Auto-billing posts charges to the patient account for every clinical action that has a corresponding price list entry. Lab request generates a lab charge. Drug dispensed generates a drug charge. Bed day generates a bed day charge. Procedure performed generates a procedure charge. No manual charge entry is required for standard services listed in the facility price list.
- BR-FIN-002: Insurance co-pay splitting applies when a patient has an active insurance scheme. The system splits the bill into two portions: the insurer portion calculated per the benefit schedule, and the patient co-pay portion. The patient pays the co-pay at the point of service. The insurer portion is posted to the insurance receivables ledger for later claim submission.
- BR-FIN-003: Mobile money reconciliation handles MTN MoMo and Airtel Money payments received via API integration. Payments are auto-matched to patient accounts using the transaction reference number. Unmatched payments are posted to a suspense account and must be manually matched by the cashier or accountant within 48 hours. The system generates a daily unmatched payments report.
- BR-FIN-004: Daily cashier reconciliation is mandatory at the end of each cashier session. Each session records: opening float, collections by payment mode (cash, MTN MoMo, Airtel Money, card), total collections, banking amount, and closing float. The system calculates expected totals and flags discrepancies exceeding UGX 5,000 for supervisor review.
- BR-FIN-005: Credit management allows facilities to extend credit to staff, corporations, and missions (NGOs, churches). All credit arrangements require prior approval from the Facility Admin. The system generates a monthly credit ageing report with buckets: 0-30 days, 31-60 days, 61-90 days, and 90+ days overdue.
- BR-FIN-006: Write-off of uncollectable debts requires a documented reason and tiered approval. Amounts under UGX 500,000 require Facility Admin approval. Amounts of UGX 500,000 or above require Facility Director approval. All write-offs are recorded in the audit trail with the approving officer, amount, patient account, and reason.
- BR-FIN-007: Deposit on admission is required for all inpatient admissions. The deposit amount is configurable per ward and per patient category. The deposit is offset against the final bill at discharge. If the final bill exceeds the deposit, the remaining balance is collected before discharge is completed. If the deposit exceeds the final bill, a refund is processed.
- BR-FIN-008: Missing charge detection runs as a daily automated report. The system compares clinical encounters (OPD visits, lab results received, drugs dispensed, procedures performed) against billing records. Encounters with no corresponding charge are flagged for review by the billing officer.

## BR-DATA: Data Rules

- BR-DATA-001: Global patient identity is maintained at the platform level. The `global_patients` table carries no `facility_id` column; one record represents one human across all facilities. Facility-level encounters, clinical notes, and billing are tenant-scoped. Facility B can see that a patient has visited Facility A (identity lookup) but cannot read clinical notes, diagnoses, or prescriptions without explicit patient consent or emergency access (BR-DATA-002).
- BR-DATA-002: Emergency access to cross-facility clinical data requires two-factor patient confirmation: patient name and date of birth. Emergency access reveals: allergies, current medications, blood group, HIV status (only if prior consent was given), and the last 3 recorded diagnoses. All emergency accesses are audited with the accessing clinician, facility, timestamp, and documented reason. The patient is notified via SMS. Emergency access expires automatically after 24 hours.
- BR-DATA-003: Offline conflict resolution uses field-level merge with a conflict log. If the same patient record is edited at two workstations while both are offline, the system merges changes by field. For non-clinical fields (address, phone, next of kin), last-write-wins applies. For clinical fields (diagnoses, prescriptions, allergies), both versions are preserved side by side and flagged for clinician review. No clinical data is silently overwritten.
- BR-DATA-004: Tenant isolation is enforced at two levels. Every tenant-scoped query includes `WHERE facility_id = ?` enforced at the Repository base class level. An Eloquent global scope provides secondary defence. Raw SQL queries that do not include tenant scope are rejected by a CI audit rule during build. No exception to this rule is permitted.
- BR-DATA-005: Auto-save protects against data loss from power failure. Every form interaction is auto-saved to local storage at configurable intervals. On form submission, local data is synced to the server. On power loss or browser crash, the last auto-saved state is recoverable when the user next opens the form. No clinical data entry is lost due to power failure.
- BR-DATA-006: Data quality enforcement requires structured input for key clinical fields. The diagnosis field requires ICD-10 code selection from a searchable list; free-text diagnosis entry is not permitted. Patient registration requires at minimum: name, sex, estimated age or date of birth, and at least one contact method (phone number or physical address). Discharge requires a completed discharge summary (BR-CLIN-009).

## BR-HMIS: HMIS Reporting Rules

- BR-HMIS-001: HMIS 105 (Outpatient Monthly Report) is auto-populated from clinical data. OPD visits auto-tally to Section 1 (diagnoses by age group and sex). Lab tests auto-tally to Section 2. Radiology examinations auto-tally to Section 3. Maternity services auto-tally to Section 4. HIV/AIDS services auto-tally to Section 5. Immunisation auto-tally to Section 6. Dental services auto-tally to Section 7. Eye services auto-tally to Section 8. No manual tallying is required.
- BR-HMIS-002: HMIS 108 (Inpatient Monthly Report) is auto-populated from IPD data. Admissions, discharges, and deaths auto-tally by diagnosis, age group, and sex. Bed occupancy rates are calculated from admission and discharge timestamps. Surgical operations are counted from theatre records.
- BR-HMIS-003: HMIS 033b (Weekly Epidemiological Surveillance Report) auto-tallies the 27 priority diseases from weekly OPD diagnoses matching Integrated Disease Surveillance and Response (IDSR) disease codes.
- BR-HMIS-004: DHIS2 submission exports HMIS data in a format compatible with the Uganda District Health Information Software 2 (DHIS2) platform. When internet connectivity is available, the system supports direct API push to the Uganda eHMIS instance (hmis2.health.go.ug). The system tracks which reporting periods have been submitted and which are pending.
- BR-HMIS-005: PEPFAR MER indicator calculation is available for PEPFAR-supported facilities. The following indicators are auto-calculated from clinical data: TX_CURR (currently on ART), TX_NEW (newly initiated on ART), TX_PVLS (viral load suppression), HTS_TST (HIV testing services), and VMMC (voluntary medical male circumcision). Calculations derive from ART dispensing records, lab results, and HIV testing logs.

## BR-PID: Patient Identity Rules

- BR-PID-001: Enterprise Master Patient Index (EMPI) matching uses probabilistic algorithms on name, date of birth, National Identification Number (NIN), and phone number. Fuzzy matching incorporates Soundex and Metaphone algorithms adapted for African naming patterns (compound surnames, clan names, spelling variations).
- BR-PID-002: Duplicate detection is triggered automatically at patient registration. The system searches existing records for potential duplicates and presents matches with a confidence score. The registrar must explicitly confirm "New Patient" to create a new record or select an existing record to continue. A confidence score exceeding 80% triggers a mandatory duplicate warning that cannot be dismissed without action.
- BR-PID-003: Patient merge allows duplicate records to be consolidated. Only the Records Officer or Facility Admin role may perform a merge. The merge preserves all clinical history, billing records, and encounters from both source records under the surviving record. Merges are reversible (unmerge) within 30 days. A full audit trail records the merge action, performing user, source records, and surviving record.
- BR-PID-004: Multiple patient identifiers are supported. Each patient may have any combination of: NIN (National Identification Number), MRN (Medical Record Number, unique per facility), passport number, UNHCR refugee ID, NHIS (National Health Insurance Scheme) number, phone number, and email address. Any stored identifier can be used for patient lookup.

## BR-INS: Insurance Rules

- BR-INS-001: Pre-authorisation is required for admissions and procedures that exceed the insurance scheme's benefit limit. The system generates a pre-authorisation request containing the diagnosis (ICD-10 coded), proposed treatment plan, and estimated cost. The request is submitted to the insurer electronically where supported, or printed for manual submission.
- BR-INS-002: Claim generation is automated. On discharge (IPD) or visit completion (OPD), the system auto-generates an insurance claim from the patient account. The claim includes: services rendered with procedure codes, ICD-10 diagnosis codes, itemised drug list with quantities, and total amount. Claim format is configurable per insurer to match each scheme's submission requirements.
- BR-INS-003: Rejection management provides visibility into claim outcomes. Rejected claims display the rejection reason from the insurer. The Insurance Clerk may correct the claim and resubmit. Rejection rates are tracked per insurer for performance analysis and follow-up.
- BR-INS-004: Insurance receivables ageing is tracked per insurer. The system maintains ageing buckets: 0-30 days, 31-60 days, 61-90 days, and 90+ days overdue. A monthly ageing report is auto-generated for the Facility Accountant and Facility Admin.

## BR-RX: Pharmacy and Prescribing Rules

- BR-RX-001: Controlled substances (Schedule I-V) require a separate narcotic register entry for every dispensing event. Each entry records: patient name, drug name and strength, quantity dispensed, prescribing doctor, dispensing pharmacist, and witness. A running balance is maintained per controlled substance. Any discrepancy between the physical count and the system balance triggers an immediate alert to the Facility Admin.
- BR-RX-002: Stock-aware prescribing displays current pharmacy stock levels on the prescription screen for each drug. If stock for a prescribed drug is zero, the system warns the prescriber at the point of prescribing and suggests available therapeutic alternatives from the same drug class.
- BR-RX-003: Tall Man Lettering is applied to look-alike/sound-alike (LASA) drug names in all drug selection interfaces. Differentiating letters are displayed in uppercase (e.g., hydrOXYzine vs hydrALAZINE) to reduce selection errors. The LASA drug list is maintainable by the pharmacy lead.

## BR-HOPE: Hope Missionary Hospital Rules

- BR-HOPE-001: Multi-site management treats Tororo and Kasanje as separate tenants with independent clinical, billing, and pharmacy data. The Medical Director accesses a consolidated Director Platform for cross-site reporting and oversight. A patient registered at Tororo can be looked up at Kasanje for identity purposes only (name, age, sex, identifiers); clinical records are not shared without consent or emergency access (BR-DATA-002).
- BR-HOPE-002: Indigent and sponsored patients are assigned a "indigent/sponsored" patient category. Clinical services are rendered and billed normally. At discharge or visit completion, a charity write-off workflow is initiated. The write-off requires a documented reason (patient assessment, sponsorship letter) and Facility Admin approval. Write-off amounts are linked to the donor fund accounting ledger for reconciliation and donor reporting.
