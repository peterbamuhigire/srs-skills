# Glossary

**Acre:** A unit of land area equal to 4,046.86 square metres. The primary area measurement unit used by Ugandan farmers.

**Agro-dealer:** A retail supplier of agricultural inputs (seeds, fertilisers, pesticides, veterinary medicines) operating at district level in Uganda.

**AI (Artificial Intelligence):** The simulation of human intelligence by computer systems, used in Kulima for pest/disease identification and farm advisory via the Claude API.

**Ankole Longhorn:** An indigenous cattle breed of western Uganda, characterised by large horns and heat tolerance. Distinct production benchmarks from exotic breeds.

**BXW (Banana Xanthomonas Wilt):** A bacterial disease affecting banana (matooke) plants in Uganda, caused by *Xanthomonas campestris* pv. *musacearum*.

**Chain of Custody:** The documented sequence of transfers of a produce batch from farm origin through collection, processing, and export, providing full traceability.

**Chwezi Core:** The custom PHP/MySQL multi-tenant SaaS framework shared across Kulima, Academia Pro, and Medic8 — providing auth, RBAC, billing, notifications, audit, and director platform infrastructure.

**Claude API:** The Anthropic REST API providing AI language model and vision capabilities, used in Kulima for pest/disease photo identification and natural language farm advisory.

**Collection Centre:** A physical location where cooperative member farmers deliver harvested produce for weighing, quality grading, and aggregation before onward sale.

**Cooperative (Franchise):** An agricultural cooperative managed within Kulima as a single franchise tenant with multiple member farms, supporting collective sales and payment distribution.

**Crop Season:** A defined period during which a specific crop variety is grown on a specific plot, from planting through harvest.

**DDS (Due Diligence Statement):** A formal declaration required under the EU Deforestation Regulation, certifying that a commodity was not produced on land deforested after 31 December 2020.

**Director Platform:** A separate administrative interface for farm owners, investors, or cooperative programme managers to view consolidated performance data across multiple farms.

**Dual-Mode Accounting:** A financial recording system offering both Simple Mode (non-technical: "Money in", "Money out") and Advanced Mode (double-entry accounting with chart of accounts, journal entries, and financial statements).

**Enterprise:** A distinct production unit within a farm — e.g., a maize crop enterprise, a dairy cattle enterprise, a poultry layer enterprise. Financial records are tracked per enterprise for profitability analysis.

**EUDR (EU Deforestation Regulation):** European Union Regulation 2023/1115 requiring that certain commodities (coffee, cocoa, timber, etc.) imported into the EU were not produced on land deforested after 31 December 2020.

**FCM (Firebase Cloud Messaging):** Google's cross-platform messaging solution used for push notifications to Android and iOS devices.

**FMIS (Farm Management Information System):** A software system for collecting, processing, storing, and disseminating farm management data to support decision-making.

**GeoJSON:** An open standard format for encoding geographic data structures (points, polygons, etc.) using JSON, used in Kulima for storing farm and plot boundaries.

**Geofence:** A virtual geographic boundary defined around a real-world area. In Kulima, used to trigger alerts when a GPS-tracked animal exits the defined perimeter.

**GlobalGAP:** A worldwide standard for Good Agricultural Practices, covering food safety, environmental sustainability, and worker welfare.

**GPS (Global Positioning System):** Satellite-based navigation system used in Kulima for farm boundary mapping, animal tracking, and location-based services.

**Hectare:** A unit of land area equal to 10,000 square metres or approximately 2.47 acres.

**HLS (HTTP Live Streaming):** An adaptive bitrate streaming protocol developed by Apple, used in Kulima's camera proxy to deliver RTSP camera streams over standard HTTP to mobile and web clients.

**IoT (Internet of Things):** A network of physical devices embedded with sensors and connectivity, used in Kulima for Jaguza ear tags, GPS collars, soil sensors, and weather stations.

**Jaguza:** A Ugandan livestock IoT company manufacturing solar-powered ear-tag devices and base units for cattle health monitoring, disease early warning, and GPS tracking.

**JWT (JSON Web Token):** A compact, URL-safe token format used for API authentication in mobile apps and external integrations.

**Kienyeji:** Local/indigenous chicken breeds in East Africa, with different production benchmarks from exotic commercial breeds.

**Kulima:** Swahili and Bantu root verb meaning "to till, to cultivate, to farm." The product name for this FMIS.

**MAAIF (Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries):** The Ugandan government ministry responsible for agricultural policy, regulation, and extension services.

**Mailo Land:** A form of land tenure unique to Uganda's Buganda region, where land is held in perpetuity under a freehold-like system established by the 1900 Buganda Agreement.

**Matooke:** East African cooking banana (*Musa* AAA-EA), a staple food crop in Uganda with specific variety tracking requirements (Mbwazirume, Nakitembe, Nfuuka).

**MFI (Microfinance Institution):** A financial institution providing small loans and savings services to low-income individuals, commonly used by Ugandan farmers.

**MoMo (Mobile Money):** MTN's mobile money transfer service, the dominant digital payment method in Uganda. Used in Kulima for subscription payments, worker wage disbursement, and marketplace transactions.

**NAADS (National Agricultural Advisory Services):** A Ugandan government programme providing agricultural extension and input subsidy services to farmers.

**NDVI (Normalised Difference Vegetation Index):** A remote sensing metric calculated from satellite imagery that indicates vegetation health. Values range from -1 to +1, with higher values indicating healthier vegetation.

**NIN (National Identification Number):** Uganda's unique citizen identification number, used in worker profiles for payroll compliance.

**NSSF (National Social Security Fund):** Uganda's mandatory social security contribution for employees, deducted from permanent staff wages.

**ONVIF (Open Network Video Interface Forum):** A global standard for IP-based physical security products, enabling interoperability between cameras from different manufacturers.

**OWC (Operation Wealth Creation):** A Ugandan government agricultural input distribution programme under the UPDF framework.

**PAYE (Pay As You Earn):** Uganda's income tax withholding system for employees, applicable to permanent farm staff.

**Plot:** A subdivided area within a farm designated for a specific use — cropland, pasture, greenhouse, poultry house, fish pond, orchard, etc.

**QR Code:** A two-dimensional barcode used in Kulima's traceability module to encode batch origin and chain of custody data for buyer verification.

**RBAC (Role-Based Access Control):** An access control method where system permissions are assigned based on user roles (Farm Owner, Farm Manager, Worker, Director, etc.).

**Room:** Android's persistence library providing an abstraction layer over SQLite, used in Kulima's offline-first mobile architecture for local data storage.

**RTSP (Real-Time Streaming Protocol):** A network protocol for controlling streaming media servers, used by IP cameras. Kulima's camera proxy converts RTSP to HLS for web/mobile delivery.

**SACCO (Savings and Credit Cooperative Organisation):** A member-owned financial cooperative common in Uganda, providing savings and loan services to farmers.

**Sentinel-2:** A European Space Agency satellite constellation providing free, high-resolution multispectral imagery used for NDVI crop health analysis.

**SQLCipher:** An open-source extension to SQLite providing transparent 256-bit AES encryption of database files, used in Kulima's Android app for securing local farm data.

**SwiftData:** Apple's native data persistence framework for iOS, used in Kulima's iOS app for local farm data storage.

**Tenant:** A single customer account (individual farmer, farm business, or cooperative) in Kulima's multi-tenant architecture, with complete data isolation from other tenants.

**UCDA (Uganda Coffee Development Authority):** The Ugandan regulatory body overseeing the coffee sector, issuing farmer registration numbers used in coffee traceability.

**UEPB (Uganda Export Promotion Board):** The Ugandan government agency responsible for promoting and developing Uganda's export trade.

**UGX (Uganda Shilling):** The official currency of Uganda, the primary currency in Kulima's financial module.

**USSD (Unstructured Supplementary Service Data):** A communication protocol for mobile phones that enables interactive text-based sessions, used in Kulima as a fallback for farmers without smartphones.
