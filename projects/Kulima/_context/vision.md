# Project Vision

**Project:** Kulima
**Client Contact:** Peter Bamuhigire
**Date:** 2026-04-23

## Problem Statement

Commercial and growth-stage farms in Uganda still run the business of farming across paper notebooks, WhatsApp messages, spreadsheet files, memory, and isolated point tools. That fragmentation makes it hard to plan enterprises, control labour, manage procurement, track stock, monitor post-harvest losses, understand cash flow, prepare for buyers, and prove compliance. Farmers often record activity after the fact, but they lack a system that turns those records into timely operating decisions.

Existing farm tools tend to solve only part of the problem. Some focus on field activity records, some on IoT hardware, some on traceability, and some on finance, but few connect whole-farm planning, enterprise budgeting, operations, inventory, labour, post-harvest, sales, and compliance in one operating system designed for African farming realities. Kulima should solve the management core first, then layer advanced intelligence and hardware where it creates measurable value.

## Vision

Kulima is a multi-tenant SaaS farm operating system for mixed commercial farms, cooperatives, and ambitious smallholders. It links whole-farm planning, crop and livestock operations, labour, procurement, inventory, post-harvest handling, finance, sales, compliance, and optional intelligence across web and mobile apps. It is built Uganda-first, offline-first, mobile-money integrated, and globally extensible so that a farm owner or manager can answer five questions every day: What are we producing? What is due today? What is costing us? What is ready to sell? Are we on plan?

**Tagline:** *from the farm, for the farm*

## Methodology

Hybrid (Water-Scrum-Fall) - formal requirements and design documentation up front, agile sprints for implementation, and phase gates tied to commercial readiness. Kulima needs enough structure to produce bankable and buildable documentation, while still allowing phased product learning in the field.

**Hybrid detection note:** The project has clear Water-Scrum-Fall characteristics: strategic documents and IEEE-aligned requirements are authored before development, the product roadmap is phased, and release gates are tied to business outcomes rather than pure feature completion.

## Goals

1. Replace fragmented paper, Excel, and WhatsApp-based farm records with a single offline-capable operational ledger for Ugandan farms.
2. Give commercial farms and cooperatives whole-farm planning, enterprise budgets, labour control, procurement discipline, and stock visibility in one system.
3. Track the full operating loop for crops and livestock from plan -> task -> activity -> input usage -> harvest/production -> storage -> sale -> margin analysis.
4. Produce bank-ready, investor-ready, and buyer-ready reporting from live farm data rather than manual spreadsheet consolidation.
5. Support traceability, certification, and compliance workflows required by export-oriented agribusinesses, beginning with Uganda and expanding to regional and global markets.
6. Position AI, IoT, camera, and GPS capabilities as high-value add-ons that strengthen an already useful management core.
7. Achieve 100 paying farmers in Phase 1 and scale toward UGX 150M/month MRR by Phase 4.

Uganda -> Kenya, Tanzania, Rwanda -> Nigeria, Ghana, Zambia -> Global

## Languages at Launch

English, French, Portuguese, Luganda, Swahili, Kinyarwanda

## Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Primary Need |
|---|---|
| Individual Farmer (smallholder) | Simple records, offline-first workflows, affordable pricing, local language guidance |
| Farm Business / Company | Whole-farm planning, labour control, procurement, inventory, finance, and management reporting |
| Cooperative / NGO / Programme | Member operations, collection, compliance, aggregate planning, and payment distribution |
| Farm Director / Investor | Portfolio visibility, approval workflows, performance benchmarking, fraud reduction |
| Buyer / Off-taker | Reliable batch traceability, compliance proof, quality and origin evidence |
| Banks / SACCOs / MFIs | Standardised financial, production, and asset evidence for lending decisions |
| Extension / Veterinary Services | Better farm history, clearer referrals, and better intervention timing |
| IoT / Data Partners | A stable operating platform into which specialised data products can integrate |

## Success Criteria

1. Phase 1 MVP launched with 100 paying farmers within 6 months of development start.
2. At least 70% of commercial-farm pilot tenants maintain monthly enterprise budgets and actuals for 3 consecutive months.
3. The core management loop (plan -> assign -> execute -> record -> analyse -> sell) is operational for both crop and livestock enterprises.
4. Pilot farms can generate bank-ready financial summaries and buyer-ready operational traceability without re-entering data into spreadsheets.
5. Procurement, stock, and post-harvest records reduce unexplained stock losses to below 5% on pilot commercial farms.
6. UGX 4M/month MRR is achieved in Phase 1, with a credible path to UGX 150M/month by Phase 4.
