# Supply and Replenishment Planning

**FR-SCP-009** - The system shall generate a supply plan from the approved consensus-demand plan using current inventory positions, open supply, lead times, sourcing rules, and planning policies for the selected horizon.

**FR-SCP-010** - The supply-planning engine shall respect tenant-configured sourcing rules, minimum order quantities, lot sizes, replenishment frequencies, lead times, and preferred supply source by item-location.

**FR-SCP-011** - When available supply or capacity is insufficient to satisfy planned demand, the system shall surface constrained-supply exceptions showing item, location, shortage quantity, constrained resource or source, and first projected shortage date.

**FR-SCP-012** - The system shall generate replenishment recommendations in the form of planned purchase, planned production, or planned transfer proposals, with each recommendation linked to its originating demand-plan and policy version.

**FR-SCP-013** - During constrained-supply conditions, the system shall support allocation rules based on service class, customer priority, or tenant-defined segment priority, and it shall show the quantity allocated and quantity deferred by rule.

**FR-SCP-014** - The system shall provide a planner exception workbench through which authorised users can filter, prioritise, assign, comment on, and resolve shortage, capacity, late-supply, or policy-violation exceptions.

**FR-SCP-015** - When a supply-plan version is released, the system shall export or publish only the approved recommendations to downstream execution modules; it shall not auto-create execution transactions without a release action.

**FR-SCP-016** - The system shall preserve plan-version history such that each supply-plan run records its parent demand-plan version, policy version, planning horizon, and release status.

**FR-SCP-035** - The replenishment engine shall calculate recommended order quantity using at minimum forecast demand over the planning horizon, safety stock, current on-hand quantity, reserved quantity, inbound open supply, minimum order quantity, order multiple, and tenant-defined maximum stock where configured.

**FR-SCP-036** - The system shall score each supplier-item relationship using on-time delivery rate, fill rate, defect rate, lead-time variability, price variance, and last-review outcome, and shall show the supplier score beside every planned purchase recommendation.

**FR-SCP-037** - When the recommended replenishment action is transfer rather than purchase, the system shall show the source location, available quantity after reservations, expected transport lead time, and risk of causing a stockout at the source location.

**FR-SCP-038** - The system shall support recommendation action types `buy`, `make`, `transfer`, `discount`, `hold`, and `investigate`, and each recommendation shall carry source signals, confidence, expected service impact, and expected working-capital impact.

## MRP and Constrained Supply Planning Extension

**FR-SCP-043** - The system shall run material requirements planning for manufactured and purchased item-locations by exploding released BOMs and routing-effective production plans into time-phased gross requirements, then netting those requirements against on-hand stock, reserved stock, scheduled receipts, firm planned orders, safety stock, scrap factors, and lead-time offsets.

**FR-SCP-044** - Each planned order generated by the MRP run shall retain pegging links to the demand, parent production order, BOM revision, routing revision, inventory-policy version, and planning bucket that created the requirement, so planners can trace a planned buy or make recommendation back to its source.

**FR-SCP-045** - The system shall support lot-sizing rules per item-location, including lot-for-lot, fixed order quantity, minimum order quantity, order multiple, period order quantity, and maximum stock cap; the planning result shall show which rule changed the raw net requirement into the recommended order quantity.

**FR-SCP-046** - The system shall maintain planning time fences for finished goods and strategic components: frozen horizon, controlled-change horizon, and open-planning horizon. Changes inside the frozen horizon shall require override permission, reason code, and owner approval before a released plan can be changed.

**FR-SCP-047** - The supply-planning workbench shall show exception messages for shortage, excess, expedite, defer, cancel, reschedule-in, reschedule-out, invalid lead time, missing BOM, missing routing, expired supplier, and capacity overload conditions, with an owner and due date for each unresolved exception.

**FR-SCP-048** - When finite-capacity planning is enabled, the system shall compare planned production load against work-centre calendars, standard setup time, run time, queue time, and maintenance blocks, and shall mark planned orders as feasible, overload, or unscheduled with the constrained work centre identified.

**FR-SCP-049** - The system shall allow planners to run what-if supply scenarios using alternative demand versions, supplier lead times, capacity calendars, lot-sizing policies, or service targets without changing the released plan, and shall preserve the scenario objective, assumptions, result metrics, and decision outcome.
