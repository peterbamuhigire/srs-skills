# Module 15: AI Intelligence — Functional Requirements

## 3.1 FR-AI-001: Cash Flow Forecasting

**FR-AI-001:** When the nightly cash flow forecast job runs for a tenant with at least 90 days of transaction history, the system shall retrieve all open AR invoices grouped by due date with the customer's historical average days-to-pay as a payment probability factor, all AP invoices grouped by due date, the next scheduled payroll run date and net payroll amount from the HR module, all loan repayment schedule records, and all tax remittance dates from the tenant's tax calendar. The system shall compute a projected daily net cash position for each of the next 90 calendar days under three scenarios: (a) Best Case — all AR collected on due date, all AP paid on the last allowable date; (b) Base Case — AR collected at the customer's historical average days-to-pay, AP paid on due date; (c) Worst Case — AR collected 30 days beyond due date, AP paid on due date. The system shall store all 270 projection records (3 scenarios × 90 days) in `ai_cashflow_forecast` with tenant ID, scenario, date, opening balance, projected inflows, projected outflows, and closing balance. The Finance Director dashboard shall display the worst-case trough date and amount within 2,000 ms of query. The job shall complete for tenants with up to 10,000 open transactions within 5 minutes of the scheduled 02:00 EAT trigger.

## 3.2 FR-AI-002: GL Anomaly Detection

**FR-AI-002:** When the post-period-close anomaly scan runs after each accounting period is closed, the system shall evaluate every journal entry posted in the closed period against four anomaly classifiers: (a) *Time Anomaly* — entry posted between 22:00 and 05:00 EAT or on a gazetted public holiday; (b) *Account-Pair Anomaly* — debit/credit account combination not observed in the tenant's preceding 12 months of journal history; (c) *Round-Number Anomaly* — entry amount is a multiple of 1,000,000 UGX with no linked source document ID; (d) *Author Anomaly* — entry posted by a user whose role has not posted to one of the referenced accounts in the preceding 90 days. Each classifier shall produce a score of 0 (not triggered) or 1 (triggered). Any journal entry with a composite score ≥ 2 shall be written to `ai_anomaly_flags` with journal ID, per-classifier scores, composite score, and flag timestamp, and shall appear in the Finance Director's **Anomaly Inbox**. The Finance Director shall be able to mark each flag as "Reviewed — No Issue" or "Escalated — Investigation Required" with a mandatory reason field. Flags not reviewed within 7 calendar days shall be automatically escalated to the Managing Director role. The scan shall process up to 50,000 journal lines per period within 15 minutes of the period-close trigger.

## 3.3 FR-AI-003: Demand Forecasting and Reorder Recommendations

**FR-AI-003:** When the Sunday demand forecast job runs for a tenant with at least 90 days of inventory sales movement history, the system shall — for each active inventory item with at least one sale in the preceding 90 days — compute the 90-day weighted moving average daily demand (linear weights: most recent week receives weight 13, second most recent week receives weight 12, continuing to the oldest week at weight 1, normalised by the sum of weights), apply a seasonal index derived from the ratio of the same 13-week period's average daily demand in the preceding year to the full-year average daily demand for that year (defaulting to 1.0 if fewer than 365 days of history exist), compute projected days of stock as `current_on_hand ÷ (weighted_avg × seasonal_index)`, and compute recommended reorder quantity as `(45 × adjusted_daily_demand) − current_on_hand` floored at zero. The system shall write a forecast record to `ai_demand_forecast` with item ID, branch ID, current stock, adjusted daily demand, projected days of stock, recommended reorder quantity, and forecast timestamp. Items with projected days of stock < 21 days shall appear in the **Reorder Recommendations** panel of the Procurement module, sorted by days remaining ascending. A Procurement Officer or above shall be able to convert any recommendation to a Purchase Requisition pre-populated with the recommended quantity via a single action. The job shall process up to 5,000 item-branch combinations within 10 minutes of the scheduled Sunday 01:00 EAT trigger.

## 3.4 FR-AI-004: Debtor Default Risk Scoring

**FR-AI-004:** When a Sales module user submits a new credit sales order for a customer whose credit account is active, the system shall compute the customer's Risk Score before the order is confirmed using the following weighted scorecard: (a) average days-to-pay in the last 12 months — 0 points if ≤ 30 days, 10 points if 31–60 days, 25 points if > 60 days — weight 40%; (b) current overdue balance as a percentage of credit limit — 0 points if 0%, 10 points if 1–25%, 25 points if > 25% — weight 30%; (c) count of late-paid invoices in the last 6 months — 0 points if 0, 10 points if 1–2, 25 points if ≥ 3 — weight 20%; (d) payment trend — 0 points if improving, 10 points if stable, 25 points if worsening — weight 10%. A composite weighted score of 0–14 shall result in a Green status with no warning displayed. A score of 15–24 shall result in an Amber warning displayed on the order screen; the order may proceed without additional approval. A score of 25–100 shall result in a Red warning; the order shall be held and shall require explicit approval from a user holding the Sales Manager role or above before it is confirmed. The approval action and mandatory reason text shall be written to the audit log. Risk score computation shall complete within 1,000 ms of order submission.

## 3.5 FR-AI-005: Narrative Financial Reports

**FR-AI-005:** When the 5th calendar day of the month is reached and the preceding month's accounting period has status `Closed`, the system shall retrieve the closed period's P&L, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow statement, compare each line to the preceding month's actuals and to the same month's actuals in the prior year, identify the 3 largest positive and 3 largest negative variances versus budget, and generate a Management Commentary containing: (a) one headline sentence comparing net profit to the prior month and to the prior year same month; (b) one paragraph identifying the 3 largest revenue variances versus budget with a one-clause explanation for each; (c) one paragraph identifying the 3 largest expense variances versus budget with a one-clause explanation for each; (d) a "3 Things to Act On This Month" list derived from the variance analysis and the 90-day cash flow forecast. The commentary shall be generated using the Claude API (`claude-sonnet-4-6` model), stored as an HTML document in `ai_narratives` with period ID, generation timestamp, and review status, and delivered as a push notification to all users holding the Finance Director, Managing Director, or Board Observer role. The Finance Director shall be able to edit the generated commentary before it is published to Board Observer role recipients. The generation job shall complete within 60 seconds of trigger. For tenants without a configured Claude API key, the system shall generate a structured data summary in place of the narrative text and display a configuration prompt.
