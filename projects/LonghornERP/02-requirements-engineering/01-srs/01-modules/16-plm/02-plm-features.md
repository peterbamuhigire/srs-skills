# Module 16: Product Lifecycle Management - Functional Requirements

## 2.1 FR-PLM-001: Engineering Item Master and Revision Control

**FR-PLM-001:** When an authorised engineering user creates or updates a controlled product definition, the system shall maintain the object as an engineering item record with a unique item code, description, item class, unit of measure, lifecycle state, and revision identifier. The lifecycle state shall support at minimum `draft`, `in_review`, `released`, `superseded`, and `obsolete`. The system shall prevent direct editing of any revision whose lifecycle state is `released`; any further modification shall require creation of a new draft revision linked to the prior released revision as its predecessor. Revision retrieval by item code shall return the full revision history within 2,000 ms at P95 for items with up to 100 revisions.

## 2.2 FR-PLM-002: EBOM, MBOM, Variant, and Effectivity Governance

**FR-PLM-002:** When an authorised user defines a product structure, the system shall support both Engineering BOM (EBOM) and Manufacturing BOM (MBOM) representations linked to the same parent engineering item. Each BOM line shall store component item, quantity per parent, unit of measure, scrap factor, approved substitute list, and optional variant or option condition. Every released BOM line shall support at least 1 effectivity rule from the following set: effective date range, plant, serial-number range, or option code. When a downstream module requests a product structure for execution, the system shall resolve and return only the released BOM revision and BOM lines whose effectivity rules are valid for the requested plant and execution context. Resolution shall complete within 1,000 ms at P95 for product structures with up to 2,000 BOM lines.

## 2.3 FR-PLM-003: Engineering Change Workflow

**FR-PLM-003:** When any user proposes a controlled change to a released engineering item, BOM, routing reference, or linked technical document, the system shall require creation of an Engineering Change Request (ECR) capturing the affected object, reason for change, change category, requested effective date, impact summary, and requester identity. The ECR shall progress through the states `draft -> submitted -> impact_review -> approved` or `rejected`. Approval of an ECR shall require at minimum 2 distinct approvers: one engineering approver and one operations or quality approver. Upon approval, the system shall create an Engineering Change Order (ECO) record linked to the ECR and require the ECO to capture implementation instructions, affected revisions, effectivity rule, and rollback notes before release. All ECR and ECO state transitions shall be written to the Audit Log with actor, timestamp, prior state, new state, and reason.

## 2.4 FR-PLM-004: Technical Document and Compliance Control

**FR-PLM-004:** When a user uploads a controlled technical document linked to an engineering item or revision, the system shall require document type, title, revision label, file checksum, owner, and review status. Supported document types shall include at minimum drawing, specification, work instruction, test report, certificate, and regulatory approval. The system shall maintain a document revision history and shall prevent deletion of any document linked to a released engineering item revision. A tenant administrator shall be able to mark specific document types as mandatory for release per item class. If any mandatory document is missing or any linked compliance artefact is expired on the proposed release date, the system shall block the release action and display the blocking reason to the releasing user.

## 2.5 FR-PLM-005: New Product Introduction and Stage-Gate Governance

**FR-PLM-005:** When a tenant uses the PLM module for New Product Introduction (NPI), the system shall support an NPI record that progresses through the stages `concept -> feasibility -> design -> validation -> pilot -> released`. Each stage transition shall require completion of tenant-configurable exit criteria recorded as structured checklist items. The system shall allow attachment of budget notes, target release date, project owner, and key risk entries to the NPI record. The system shall prevent transition into `released` unless at minimum 1 released engineering item revision exists for the product, all mandatory stage-gate criteria are complete, and the release approvers have approved the final gate. Stage-gate completion status for up to 500 active NPI records shall load within 2,000 ms at P95.

## 2.6 FR-PLM-006: Digital Thread Publication to Downstream Modules

**FR-PLM-006:** When an authorised user releases an engineering item revision or ECO whose downstream publication flag is enabled, the system shall generate a controlled publication event containing tenant ID, released item revision, effective date, resolved BOM structure, approved substitutes, linked manufacturing-routing reference, and release identifier. The publication event shall be delivered atomically to the downstream synchronisation layer so that Inventory, Procurement, and Manufacturing consume the same released definition baseline. If publication to any mandatory downstream consumer fails, the system shall mark the publication state as `failed`, prevent the release from being marked `published`, and queue a retry while preserving the released engineering state. The publication attempt and its outcome shall be visible in a release log within 5 seconds of trigger.
