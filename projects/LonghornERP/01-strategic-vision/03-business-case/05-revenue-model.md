# Revenue Model and Financial Milestones

## Revenue Architecture

Longhorn ERP generates revenue from 3 streams:

- **Subscription MRR** — recurring monthly or annual subscriptions at the tier rates defined in Section 4.
- **Add-on MRR** — per-module activation fees stacked on top of base plan subscriptions.
- **Future streams (post-Phase 1)** — white-label licensing, API access tiers, and migration service fees. These are not modelled in Phase 1 projections. [CONTEXT-GAP: white-labelling policy — see GAP-015]

## Phase Revenue Milestones

### Phase 1 — Uganda (Target: UGX 56M MRR)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Market | Uganda |
| Target client count | 60–100 paying organisations |
| Average MRR per client | UGX 560,000–933,000 |
| Total MRR target | UGX 56,000,000 (~$15K) |
| Timeline | 12 months post-launch |

**Key activities:**

1. Direct sales to mid-sized trading, manufacturing, and service companies in Kampala metropolitan area.
2. Referral partnerships with accounting firms and bookkeeping practices.
3. EFRIS compliance as a lead-generation angle — target businesses receiving URA compliance notices.
4. Freemium or time-limited trial to reduce evaluation friction (trial period duration to be decided).
5. Onboard 10 anchor clients in Month 1–3 to generate case studies and referral momentum.

### Phase 2 — East Africa (Target: UGX 188M MRR)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Market | Kenya, Tanzania, Rwanda added to Uganda |
| Target client count | 250–350 paying organisations |
| Average MRR per client | UGX 537,000–752,000 |
| Total MRR target | UGX 188,000,000 (~$50K) |
| Timeline | 24 months post-launch |

**Key activities:**

1. Kenya localisation profile: Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) iTax integration, M-Pesa Daraja, Kenya Shilling (KES) pricing tier.
2. Tanzania localisation profile: Tanzania Revenue Authority (TRA) integration, KES/Tanzania Shilling (TZS) pricing.
3. Rwanda localisation: Rwanda Revenue Authority (RRA) integration, Rwandan Franc (RWF) pricing.
4. Establish channel reseller agreements in each market.
5. Launch Odoo and ERPNext migration scripts to accelerate switching (see GAP-013).

### Phase 4 — Enterprise and Global (Target: UGX 1.1B+ MRR)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|

**Key activities:**

3. ISO/IEC 27001 certification to enable sales to regulated sectors (banking, healthcare, government).
4. White-label licensing for Enterprise tenants (pending GAP-015 resolution).
5. Public marketplace for third-party add-on modules.

## Break-Even Analysis

Chwezi Core Systems estimates baseline monthly operating costs (cloud infrastructure, salaries for 2–3 developers, legal and compliance, payment processing fees) at UGX 12M–15M/month in Phase 1.

| Scenario | Clients Required | Assumption |
|---|---|---|
| Break-even on Starter clients only | 48–60 clients | UGX 250,000/month each |
| Break-even on Small Business clients only | 24–30 clients | UGX 500,000/month each |
| Break-even on mixed portfolio (realistic) | 20–25 clients | Blended average ~UGX 600,000/month |

The Phase 1 MRR target of UGX 56M represents approximately 3.7–4.7× the break-even threshold, providing operating margin to fund Phase 2 market entry without external capital at that point.

*Note: Break-even figures assume no initial capital injection. If Chwezi Core Systems raises pre-launch funding, the break-even runway extends and the Phase 1 client target may be relaxed.*
