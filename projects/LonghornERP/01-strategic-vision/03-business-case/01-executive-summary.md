# Executive Summary

Mid-sized organisations across East Africa manage their operations on spreadsheets, disconnected accounting packages, and manual registers — not because they lack the sophistication to use an Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) platform, but because every credible ERP on the market is either priced in US Dollars at rates that make monthly costs unpredictable, sold per seat so that growing the team doubles the software bill, or requires a six-figure implementation engagement before a single transaction can be posted. The result is a permanent productivity ceiling for thousands of otherwise viable businesses.

Longhorn ERP will close this gap. It is a multi-tenant, modular Software as a Service (SaaS) ERP platform priced in Ugandan Shillings (UGX) on an organisation-wide basis — one flat monthly fee covering every user in the organisation. It ships 16 universal modules, integrates natively with Uganda Revenue Authority (URA) Electronic Fiscal Receipting and Invoicing System (EFRIS), MTN Mobile Money (MoMo), Airtel Money, and M-Pesa, and enforces a zero-mandatory-training design standard: if a user needs a manual to complete a task, the interface is redesigned until they do not. It is the flagship product of Chwezi Core Systems and the primary commercial vehicle for the company.

Uganda alone holds an estimated 8,000–12,000 formally registered mid-sized enterprises that operate without an integrated ERP. East Africa as a whole — including Kenya, Tanzania, Rwanda, South Sudan, and Burundi — represents a serviceable addressable market of approximately 50,000–80,000 organisations at the Starter-to-Professional price tier. Longhorn ERP targets a 0.5% capture of the Ugandan market within 12 months of launch (60–100 clients, UGX 56 million Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)), scaling to an East Africa footprint of 500+ clients and UGX 188 million MRR by the end of Phase 2.
