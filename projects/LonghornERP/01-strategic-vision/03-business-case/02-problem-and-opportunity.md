# The ERP Gap in East Africa

## The Problem

Four structural barriers prevent mid-sized East African organisations from adopting modern ERP platforms.

**1. Prohibitive cost.** Every tier-1 ERP is priced for the USD-denominated economies of North America and Western Europe. At the exchange rate of approximately UGX 3,750 per USD prevailing in early 2026, the costs are as follows for a 30-user organisation:

| Platform | Approximate Cost (30 users) |
|---|---|
| SAP Business One | ~UGX 145M licence + UGX 110M–375M implementation |
| Oracle NetSuite | ~UGX 14.9M/month |
| Microsoft Dynamics 365 Business Central | ~UGX 7.9M–11.3M/month + implementation |
| Odoo Enterprise | ~UGX 4.3M/month + UGX 75M–300M implementation |
| ERPNext | Free software + UGX 56M–188M to implement |
| **Longhorn ERP** | **UGX 250,000–2,500,000/month, no implementation fee** |

**2. Per-seat pricing penalises growth.** Odoo and Microsoft Dynamics 365 charge per active user. A trading company that scales from 10 to 30 staff sees its ERP bill triple. This structure creates a direct financial disincentive against formalising roles and adding system users.

**3. No Africa-native integrations.** SAP, Oracle, and Dynamics do not integrate with URA EFRIS, MTN MoMo, Airtel Money, M-Pesa, or the NSSF Uganda employer portal out of the box. Each integration requires a custom development engagement costing UGX 10M–50M or more per connector.

**4. USD-denominated pricing creates exchange rate anxiety.** When the Uganda Shilling depreciates against the US Dollar — as it did by approximately 8% in 2023 — a company's ERP subscription cost rises by the same proportion without any change in usage. Finance teams cannot budget with confidence.

## Why Now

3 converging forces make this the right moment to launch.

**URA EFRIS mandate.** Uganda Revenue Authority has mandated EFRIS-compliant invoicing for VAT-registered taxpayers. Every business that issues tax invoices must integrate with EFRIS or face penalties. An ERP with native EFRIS support removes this compliance burden entirely — and creates an immediate, compliance-driven sales argument.

**Mobile money infrastructure maturity.** MTN MoMo and Airtel Money collectively serve over 30 million registered mobile money accounts in Uganda as of 2024. Business-to-business and business-to-employee payment via mobile money is now standard operating practice. An ERP that cannot post, reconcile, and report on mobile money transactions is incomplete by default.

**Post-COVID digitisation mandate.** The COVID-19 period exposed the operational fragility of paper-based and spreadsheet-driven businesses. A measurable shift toward digital recordkeeping and cloud-based tools has occurred across East Africa since 2021, with NITA-U reporting accelerated SaaS adoption across public and private sector organisations in Uganda.

## The Opportunity

Uganda's Uganda Bureau of Statistics (UBOS) 2020/21 Business Register records approximately 150,000 formally registered enterprises. Of these, an estimated 8,000–12,000 qualify as mid-sized by revenue (UGX 500M–50B/year) and employee count (10–200 staff) — the primary Longhorn ERP target segment. At an average Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) of UGX 600,000 per client (approximately the Small Business tier), even a 1% capture of this segment generates UGX 48M–72M MRR. The Phase 1 target of UGX 56M MRR requires 60–100 paying clients — a credible pipeline for a well-executed sales motion over 12 months.

Across East Africa, the serviceable addressable market expands to an estimated 50,000–80,000 organisations at comparable revenue and staff scale.
