# Lean Canvas — Longhorn ERP

This document presents the Lean Canvas for Longhorn ERP, structured across the 9 standard blocks of the Lean Canvas framework (Maurya, 2012). Each block captures the prospective business model hypothesis that will be validated through phased market entry.

---

## 1. Problem

The 3 highest-priority problems that Longhorn ERP will address:

- Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) software is unaffordable for East African Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs). SAP Business One carries a licence cost of approximately UGX 145M plus UGX 110M–375M in implementation fees. Oracle NetSuite runs approximately UGX 14.9M/month. Even Odoo Enterprise costs approximately UGX 4.3M/month before implementation. These prices place ERPs 4–100× above what a Ugandan SME can sustain.
- Implementation requires expensive third-party consultants. The typical Ugandan implementation of an off-the-shelf ERP costs UGX 75M–375M in consulting fees alone — often exceeding the software licence — and takes 6–18 months to complete. Smaller organisations cannot absorb this cost or timeline.
- No commercially available ERP natively integrates with Uganda's Electronic Fiscal Receipting and Invoicing System (EFRIS), MTN MoMo, or Airtel Money. Organisations must build or commission custom bridges, each adding cost, maintenance risk, and compliance uncertainty.

## 2. Customer Segments

### Early Adopters

- Uganda manufacturing companies (food processing, agro-processing, beverages, dairy, flour milling) that currently run on Microsoft Excel or QuickBooks and have outgrown both.
- Finance managers and System Administrators at organisations with 5–30 employees who make the software-purchasing decision and are actively seeking an ERP alternative.

### Mass Market

- All mid-sized organisations in Uganda with 5–500 employees, across all sectors: manufacturing, trading and distribution, retail, professional services, hospitality, Non-Governmental Organisations (NGOs), and cooperatives.
- East African NGOs and donor-funded organisations requiring project accounting, logframe reporting, and multi-currency financials.
- Agricultural cooperatives managing farmer intake, mobile money payments, and input loan deductions.
- Retail chains requiring a Point of Sale (POS) terminal integrated with back-office inventory and accounting.

## 3. Unique Value Proposition

**Headline:** "The ERP your team can use without training."

**Sub-headline:** "From UGX 250,000/month for the whole organisation. No consultants. No per-seat fees. Built for East Africa."

The core promise is the Golden Rule: if a user would need a manual or a consultant to complete a task, the interface will be redesigned until they do not. This guarantee — enforced by a ≥ 85% unassisted task success rate target — is the differentiating commitment no incumbent ERP vendor makes.

---

## 4. Solution

The 3 features that directly address the problems identified in Block 1:

- **Org-wide subscription pricing from UGX 250,000/month.** All 16 modules are available from the Starter tier. No per-seat fees. A 50-person company pays the same as a 5-person company at the same tier, removing the affordability barrier entirely.
- **Zero-training User Experience (UX) enforced by the Golden Rule.** Every workflow will be tested against a ≥ 85% unassisted task success threshold before release. No feature will ship if it requires a manual to operate. This removes the consultant dependency for day-to-day use.
- **Built-in EFRIS, MTN MoMo, Airtel Money, and M-Pesa integrations from day one.** These integrations will be part of the platform Integration Layer — not optional add-ons — so every tenant benefits from compliant, real-time fiscal and payment connectivity without custom development.

## 5. Channels

The channels through which Longhorn ERP will acquire, activate, and retain customers:

- **Direct sales** — The Chwezi sales team will handle Enterprise and Business-tier prospects through discovery calls, demos, and assisted onboarding.
- **Website self-signup** — Starter and Small Business tier customers will onboard via chwezicore.com without sales assistance. The self-service flow will include a 14-day free trial.
- **Accountant and consultant referral programme** — Chartered accountants, bookkeepers, and IT consultants who recommend Longhorn ERP to their clients will earn a recurring referral commission. This channel targets the people who currently advise SMEs on financial software choices.
- **Integration with Uganda's business registration ecosystem** — Partnerships with Uganda Registration Services Bureau (URSB) and URA onboarding journeys will position Longhorn ERP as the recommended accounting and compliance tool at the point of business formation.
- **Africa's Talking and WhatsApp for onboarding communication** — Onboarding sequences, payment reminders, and support escalations will use WhatsApp Business API and Africa's Talking SMS, meeting users on the channels they already use daily.

## 6. Revenue Streams

The revenue mechanisms that will fund Longhorn ERP operations and growth:

- **Monthly subscriptions** — Recurring UGX-denominated subscription fees across 5 tiers: Starter (UGX 250,000/month), Small Business (UGX 500,000/month), Professional (UGX 1,000,000/month), Business (UGX 1,750,000/month), and Enterprise (UGX 2,500,000/month).
- **Annual billing plans** — Tenants who pay annually will receive 12 months of access billed at 10 months' cost, generating upfront cash flow and reducing monthly churn risk.
- **A-la-carte add-on modules** — Small Business and Professional tier tenants may activate individual add-on modules (e.g., HR & Payroll at UGX 150,000/month, Manufacturing at UGX 250,000/month) above their base plan.
- **Custom Enterprise plans** — Large organisations with negotiated terms, dedicated infrastructure, white-labelling requirements, or non-standard support Service Level Agreements (SLAs) will be invoiced under custom commercial agreements.

## 7. Cost Structure

The primary cost categories that will determine unit economics and pricing sustainability:

- **Cloud hosting and infrastructure** — Multi-tenant application servers, managed database clusters, object storage, Content Delivery Network (CDN), and backup infrastructure.
- **Development team** — Salaries or contract fees for 2–5 full-stack PHP + MySQL developers and mobile developers for Android (Kotlin/Jetpack Compose) and iOS (Swift/SwiftUI).
- **Integration fees** — URA EFRIS API access, MTN MoMo Developer fees, Airtel Money API costs, Africa's Talking SMS/USSD credits, and KRA iTax connection fees.
- **Sales and marketing** — Website hosting, digital advertising, content production, referral programme payouts, and conference attendance.
- **Customer support** — Support staff, helpdesk tooling, and knowledge base authoring.
- **Legal and compliance** — Uganda Data Protection and Privacy Act 2019 (DPPA) compliance audits, National Information Technology Authority Uganda (NITA-U) SaaS registration, and commercial legal review.

## 8. Key Metrics

The quantitative indicators that will determine whether the business model is working:

- **Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) in UGX** — Target: UGX 56M by the end of Phase 1 (Uganda market). This is the primary commercial health indicator.
- **Monthly Active Tenants** — Count of tenants who log at least 1 session per month. Declining active tenants signal churn risk before it appears in revenue.
- **Monthly churn rate** — Target: ≤ 5% monthly tenant churn. At ≤ 5%, average tenant lifetime exceeds 20 months and the business achieves positive unit economics.
- **Time to first productive use** — Target: ≤ 1 business day from account creation to first live transaction processed. Measures the effectiveness of the self-serve onboarding flow.
- **Support ticket volume per tenant per month** — Target: decreasing month-on-month as the product matures. High ticket volume is a UX failure signal.
- **Golden Rule compliance rate** — Target: ≥ 85% unassisted task success across core workflows in usability testing. This is the primary UX health metric and the operational definition of the platform's central promise.

## 9. Unfair Advantage

The assets and capabilities that will be difficult for competitors to copy or acquire:

- **First ERP natively built for Uganda's full regulatory stack.** Longhorn ERP will ship with native, tested integrations for EFRIS, Uganda Revenue Authority (URA) PAYE e-returns, National Social Security Fund (NSSF) Uganda employer upload, and Public Procurement and Disposal of Public Assets Authority (PPDA) compliance workflows. No equivalent product exists in the Ugandan market with all of these built in from launch.
- **Configuration-driven Localisation Engine.** Adding a new country or jurisdiction will require only a localisation configuration profile — no code changes, no separate product variant. This means the platform can enter a new East African or Francophone African market in weeks rather than years, compounding the first-mover advantage.
- **Part of the Chwezi Core product family.** Longhorn ERP shares infrastructure, user management, and billing systems with Academia Pro (school management), Medic8 (healthcare management), and Kulima (farm management). Cross-product integrations — such as a school billing into Longhorn ERP accounting, or a farm cooperative paying farmers via Longhorn ERP payroll — create switching costs and network effects that standalone competitors cannot replicate.
- **Peter Bamuhigire's domain expertise.** The Lead Architect combines direct experience in East African ERP implementations, SaaS architecture, and Uganda's regulatory environment. This knowledge is embedded in product decisions, not recoverable by copying the feature set.
