# Target Market

## Design Principle: Industry-Agnostic by Design

Longhorn ERP is not a vertical ERP. All modules are universal. Industry-specific behaviour — commodity types, regulatory workflows, statutory integrations — is delivered through the tenant's configuration profile and the Localisation Engine, not through industry-specific module variants. A manufacturing company and a law firm activate different modules from the same codebase.

## Primary Market: Uganda

Uganda is the launch market. The platform's pricing is denominated in Ugandan Shillings. Phase 1 integrations target Uganda's statutory systems: URA EFRIS, URA PAYE e-returns, NSSF Uganda, NITA-U compliance.

## Priority Sectors

The following sectors represent the highest-density opportunity in Uganda and are the primary targets for Phase 1 sales and onboarding:

- Manufacturing: food processing, agro-processing, beverages, dairy, flour milling
- Trading and distribution: Fast-Moving Consumer Goods (FMCG) distributors, hardware wholesalers, pharmaceutical distributors
- Agricultural cooperatives: tea, coffee, sugarcane, cotton, matooke, outgrower schemes
- Service businesses: consulting firms, law practices, accounting firms, NGOs
- Retail and point of sale: supermarkets, pharmacies, hardware shops
- Hospitality: hotels, lodges, restaurants, bars
- NGOs and donor-funded organisations
- Government and parastatals (Public Procurement and Disposal of Public Assets Authority (PPDA) compliance)
- Professional services: engineering firms, construction companies, architects
- Schools and universities (with integration to Academia Pro)

## Ideal Customer Profile

| Attribute | Target Range |
|---|---|
| Employee count | 5–500 |
| Monthly revenue | UGX 10M–10B |
| IT capability | Non-technical owner or small IT team (1–3 people) |
| Current state | Operating on spreadsheets, QuickBooks, or a partially deployed legacy ERP |
| Decision maker | CEO, Finance Director, or Operations Manager |
| Geography | Uganda (Phase 1); East Africa (Phase 2) |

## Market Size Rationale

Uganda has approximately 500,000 registered businesses, of which an estimated 40,000–80,000 fall within the 5–500 employee band that constitutes the ideal customer profile. Penetrating 1% of this addressable market (400–800 tenants) at an average monthly revenue of UGX 700,000 per tenant yields UGX 280M–560M MRR, which exceeds the Phase 1 MRR target of UGX 56M by a factor of 5–10.
