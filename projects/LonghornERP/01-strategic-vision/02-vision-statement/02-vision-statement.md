# Vision Statement — Longhorn ERP Market Dominance

## 5–10 Year Vision

**Longhorn ERP shall become the default Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) platform for mid-sized and growing organisations in East and Central Africa — the system that accountants, operations managers, and business owners reach for first, without needing a salesperson to convince them and without needing a consultant to configure it.**

Within 10 years of launch, Longhorn ERP shall hold a recognised position as the highest-volume ERP platform by active tenants across the East and Central African region, achieving this position not by competing on features alone but by delivering a total cost of ownership (TCO) that no international competitor can match at the mid-market tier, combined with Africa-native integrations — Uganda Revenue Authority (URA) Electronic Fiscal Receipting and Invoicing Solution (EFRIS), MTN Mobile Money (MoMo), Airtel Money, M-Pesa Daraja — that international platforms treat as afterthoughts.

The platform shall be industry-agnostic by architecture. Any organisation with 5 to 500 employees — a flour mill in Jinja, a pharmaceutical distributor in Nairobi, a coffee cooperative in Gitega, a consulting firm in Dakar — shall be able to activate Longhorn ERP, configure it for their jurisdiction via localisation profile, and begin productive operations on the same day.

## Strategic Milestones Underpinning the Vision

The following 8 goals define the measurable commitments that shall transform the vision into an auditable delivery record:

1. Dominate the Uganda ERP market as the default choice for mid-sized organisations before Phase 2 launch.
2. Expand across East Africa (Kenya, Tanzania, Rwanda) within 24 months of Uganda launch.
3. Enter Francophone Africa (DRC, Cameroon, Côte d'Ivoire, Senegal, Mali, Burkina Faso) within 36 months.
4. Achieve global configurability — any country addable via localisation profile with no code changes.
5. Surpass Odoo and ERPNext on unassisted task success rate; surpass SAP and Oracle on price-to-capability ratio.
6. Integrate with every major mobile money provider in East and Central Africa.
7. Deliver native mobile applications (Android and iOS) feeding the same ERP backend.
8. Expose a versioned public Application Programming Interface (API) so that external systems can integrate with Longhorn ERP without bespoke middleware.
