# Market Positioning — Longhorn ERP

## Where Longhorn ERP Does Not Compete

Longhorn ERP shall not compete at the low end of the accounting software market. Products such as QuickBooks, Wave, and Sage 50 serve micro-businesses — sole traders, small partnerships, and organisations with fewer than 5 employees — that require bookkeeping and invoicing only. These platforms offer no procurement workflow, no inventory management, no manufacturing or cooperative processing, no payroll beyond basic salary calculations, and no multi-branch or multi-currency capability. Longhorn ERP is built for organisations that have outgrown these tools.

Longhorn ERP shall also not compete with enterprise ERP behemoths — SAP S/4HANA, Oracle E-Business Suite, and Microsoft Dynamics 365 Finance — whose implementations are measured in years, whose licence costs are measured in hundreds of millions of Ugandan Shillings (UGX), and whose user populations are large, technically trained, and supported by dedicated IT departments. Those platforms are designed for organisations with 500 to 50,000 employees and the budget to match.

## The Space Longhorn ERP Occupies

Longhorn ERP occupies the mid-market tier: organisations with 5 to 500 employees that require genuine enterprise capability — multi-module integration, a complete audit trail, statutory compliance, mobile money disbursements, and multi-branch operations — but cannot afford, and should not need, a consulting firm to deploy and maintain their ERP.

The target IT maturity profile is low to medium. Longhorn ERP shall not require a dedicated IT team, a database administrator, or a systems integrator. A business owner, a finance manager, or an operations director shall be able to provision a tenant, configure the localisation profile, and begin productive operations without escalating to technical support.

## Target Organisational Profile

- Employee count: 5 to 500
- IT maturity: low to medium — no dedicated IT team required
- Sectors: manufacturing, trading and distribution, agricultural cooperatives, services, retail, hospitality, non-governmental organisations (NGOs), government and parastatals, professional services, schools and universities
- Geography: Uganda at launch; East Africa within 24 months; Francophone Africa and global within 60 months
- Price sensitivity: monthly spend must be comparable to operational overheads — UGX 250,000 to UGX 2,500,000/month for the whole organisation, not per user

## Formal Positioning Statement

*Longhorn ERP is the professionally capable, radically affordable, zero-training mid-market ERP built for organisations in East and Central Africa that have outgrown accounting tools but cannot justify the cost or complexity of SAP, Oracle, or Odoo.* Longhorn ERP shall deliver the full functional depth of enterprise software — accounting, inventory, procurement, sales, manufacturing, HR and payroll, point of sale, project management, and cooperative processing — at a flat organisational price that a 30-person company in Kampala can meet from operating cash flow, with Africa-native integrations active from Day 1 and no implementation fee, no training obligation, and no per-seat escalation as the organisation grows.
