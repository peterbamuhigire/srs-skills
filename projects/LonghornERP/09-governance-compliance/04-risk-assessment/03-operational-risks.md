# Operational and Architectural Risk Register (RISK-019 – RISK-024)

These risks are not derived from open design gaps. They arise from architectural decisions, infrastructure dependencies, and staffing constraints identified during the platform design review.

## 3.1 Risk Entries

| Risk ID | Source | Description | Category | Probability | Impact | Score | Mitigation | Owner | Target Date |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| RISK-019 | Architecture review | URA EFRIS API unavailability prevents invoice submission. If the URA endpoint is down, fiscal invoices cannot be generated and sales workflows are blocked for all tenants simultaneously. | Operational | M | H | **High (6)** | Implement a queue-and-retry mechanism in the EFRIS integration layer. Invoices that fail submission are stored with status `PENDING_EFRIS`. The system retries automatically every 15 minutes using an exponential back-off up to 4 hours. After 2 consecutive hours of failure, send an alert to the system administrator via email and in-app notification. Log all retry attempts with HTTP status codes and timestamps for audit purposes. | Peter Bamuhigire | 2026-06-01 |
| RISK-020 | Architecture review | MTN MoMo or Airtel Money API breaking changes introduced without advance notice render salary disbursement and bulk payment workflows non-functional. | Integration | M | H | **High (6)** | Implement all mobile money provider calls through a single `MobileMoneyService` abstraction layer. No module calls a provider SDK directly. Subscribe to MTN MoMo and Airtel API changelog RSS feeds and developer newsletter. Run automated smoke tests against the payment sandbox every calendar month. When a breaking change is detected, the abstraction layer is the only file requiring update, limiting blast radius to 1 class. | Peter Bamuhigire | 2026-06-15 |
| RISK-021 | Legal review gap | Employee, farmer, and student personally identifiable information (PII) may be stored without legally compliant consent recording, retention limits, or sub-processor disclosure under the Uganda Data Protection and Privacy Act 2019. Non-compliance exposes Chwezi Core Systems to investigation and fines by the Personal Data Protection Office. | Regulatory | M | H | **High (6)** | Complete the legal review required by GAP-007 (RISK-007). Implement consent recording at the point of data collection: every PII field must trace to a recorded consent event with timestamp and version of the privacy policy accepted. Enforce a 7-year data retention limit for financial and HR records; after the retention period, records are archived to cold storage and purged from the active database. Publish a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) for all tenants before commercial go-live. | Peter Bamuhigire | 2026-07-01 |
| RISK-022 | Architecture review | A bug in the multi-tenancy scoping layer causes cross-tenant data leakage — one tenant's records become visible to another tenant's users. Even a single confirmed leakage incident constitutes a data breach requiring notification under the Uganda Data Protection and Privacy Act 2019. | Technical | L | H | **Medium (4)** | Complete the independent multi-tenancy security review per GAP-004 (RISK-004). Enforce `tenant_id` as a mandatory WHERE clause parameter in every SQL query that targets a tenanted table, validated by a PHPStan custom rule at level 8 that fails the build on violation. Conduct penetration testing against a staging environment with 2 test tenants before any live tenant is onboarded. All penetration test findings rated Medium or above must be remediated before go-live sign-off. Monitor this risk closely; probability is Low but impact is catastrophic if realised. | Peter Bamuhigire | 2026-06-01 |
| RISK-023 | Financial modelling | USD-denominated infrastructure costs (hosting, CDN, API fees) rise while Longhorn ERP subscription pricing is fixed in UGX. Exchange rate depreciation erodes gross margin without a corresponding revenue adjustment mechanism. | Strategic | M | M | **Medium (4)** | Implement an annual price review mechanism. UGX subscription prices are reviewed on July 1 each year and adjusted to maintain a minimum 65% gross margin. Enterprise contracts above UGX 1,000,000/month are available in USD denomination to eliminate FX exposure on large accounts. Document the pricing review procedure in the Business Case (`01-strategic-vision/03-business-case`). | Peter Bamuhigire | 2026-07-01 |
| RISK-024 | Staffing constraint | Peter Bamuhigire is the sole developer. Illness, personal emergency, or competing obligations halt all development and support activity. There is no secondary developer capable of maintaining the codebase in the event of unavailability. | Operational | M | H | **High (6)** | Maintain comprehensive SDLC documentation (this document set) so the codebase can be handed off to a second developer with minimal ramp-up time. Enforce PHPStan level 8 and PSR-12 throughout so the codebase is readable without institutional knowledge. Produce an onboarding guide for a second developer by Q3 2026. Begin recruitment or contractor engagement for a second developer by 2026-07-01. Until a second developer is onboarded, avoid committing to client SLAs that require < 24-hour incident response. | Peter Bamuhigire | 2026-09-30 |
