## 3.4 F-018: AI Intelligence Module (Phase 7)

> *Phase 7 scope. These FRs shall not block Phase 6 acceptance. They are included here to establish traceability and technical specification for the contract extension.*

---

### 3.4.1 FR-AI-001: Production Yield Prediction

**FR-AI-001**
When a production order is moved to `in_progress` status and at least one raw material batch with a recorded quality grade (A, B, or C) has been allocated to the order, the system shall retrieve the quality grade distribution of the allocated batches by weight percentage, apply the configured yield coefficient matrix — Grade A: nominal yield × 1.0; Grade B: nominal yield × 0.93; Grade C: nominal yield × 0.85, all three coefficients configurable by the Production Director without developer involvement (DC-002) — compute the predicted output tonnage as the sum of each grade's allocated weight multiplied by the recipe's nominal yield multiplied by that grade's coefficient, write the prediction to `ai_yield_predictions` with production order ID, allocated batch IDs, grade distribution percentages, predicted output tonnage, configured coefficients used, and prediction timestamp, and display the predicted output on the production order screen alongside the recipe's nominal output. If predicted output differs from the committed output quantity by more than 5%, the system shall display a non-blocking Yield Alert: "Predicted yield is [X]% below committed output. Review batch mix before proceeding." The system shall not prevent the production order from proceeding; the alert is informational only. Prediction computation shall complete within 2,000 ms of the status change event.

### 3.4.2 FR-AI-002: Quality Defect Pattern Detection

**FR-AI-002**
When a quality inspection result is saved for a completed production batch and at least 3 prior inspection records exist for the same product and quality parameter within the preceding 90 days, the system shall retrieve the 5 most recent inspection results for that product-parameter combination in chronological order, compute the mean and standard deviation of those 5 values, evaluate Western Electric Rule 1 (any single point beyond 3 standard deviations from the mean) and Western Electric Rule 2 (3 consecutive points beyond 2 standard deviations from the mean in the same direction), and — if either rule is triggered — create an `ai_process_alert` record with product ID, parameter code, rule triggered, the 5 measurement values, computed mean, computed standard deviation, alert timestamp, and review status defaulting to Unreviewed. The system shall dispatch an in-app alert to all users holding the Quality Manager or Production Director role within 5 minutes of alert creation. The alert text shall state: "Quality parameter [parameter name] on [product name] has triggered Control Chart [Rule 1 / Rule 2] across the last [N] batches. Review recommended." The Quality Manager shall be able to mark the alert Reviewed — Root Cause Identified (with mandatory root cause text entry, minimum 20 characters) or Reviewed — No Action Required (with mandatory reason text entry).

### 3.4.3 FR-AI-003: Farmer Supply Forecasting

**FR-AI-003**
When the quarterly supply forecast job runs on the 1st calendar day of each quarter — January, April, July, October — and the system contains at least 12 months of cooperative delivery history, the system shall — for each active cooperative — retrieve all delivery records for the corresponding calendar quarter in each prior year of available history, compute the mean and standard deviation of total quarterly delivery tonnage per cooperative across those years, apply an optional rainfall adjustment factor if the Open-Meteo historical rainfall total for the Bushenyi GPS coordinates (latitude −0.5833, longitude 30.2167) in the same quarter of the prior year deviates from the multi-year quarterly mean by more than 20% (adjustment factor configurable by the Procurement Manager: range 0.7–1.3, default 1.0 when no deviation threshold is exceeded), compute the forecast tonnage as mean × rainfall adjustment factor and the confidence interval as mean ± 1 standard deviation, and write a forecast record to `ai_supply_forecast` with cooperative ID, quarter identifier, forecast tonnage, lower bound, upper bound, rainfall adjustment applied (boolean and factor value), and forecast timestamp. The system shall display the quarterly supply forecast table in the Procurement module accessible to the Procurement Manager role and above. The forecast job shall complete within 10 minutes of the quarterly trigger for up to 200 active cooperatives. For cooperatives with fewer than 12 months of delivery history, the system shall write a record with forecast status `insufficient_history` and display "Insufficient history" in the forecast table rather than a tonnage figure.

### 3.4.4 FR-AI-004: Predictive Equipment Maintenance

**FR-AI-004**
When the weekly maintenance prediction scan runs every Monday at 06:00 EAT and an asset registered in the Asset Management module has at least 3 completed service records, the system shall compute the average interval between consecutive services in production hours (if runtime hours are logged) or calendar days (if runtime hours are not available), compute the production hours or calendar days elapsed since the most recent completed service for that asset, compute the Maintenance Proximity Score as `(elapsed ÷ average_interval) × 100` rounded to the nearest integer, and — if the score exceeds 80 — write a prediction record to `ai_maintenance_predictions` with asset ID, asset name, score, last service date, average service interval, projected next service date (last service date + average interval), and prediction timestamp, and create an in-app alert for all users holding the Maintenance Manager role: "Asset [name] (ID: [asset_id]) is at [score]% of its average maintenance interval. Recommended service window: [projected date − 3 days] to [projected date + 3 days]." The system shall not automatically create a maintenance work order; the alert is informational only. The Maintenance Manager shall be able to mark the alert Acknowledged — Scheduled (with mandatory scheduled date entry) or Acknowledged — Deferred (with mandatory reason and deferred date entry). The weekly scan shall complete within 15 minutes of the Monday 06:00 EAT trigger for up to 100 tracked assets.

### 3.4.5 FR-AI-005: Export Demand Intelligence

**FR-AI-005**
When a user holding the Sales Manager or Export Manager role saves a new export order record, the system shall perform a Production Feasibility Check within 5,000 ms: retrieve all confirmed production orders with completion dates in the 8-week window preceding the export order's required shipment date, sum the committed output tonnage already scheduled in that window for the same product, compute available production capacity as `(configured_daily_production_capacity × working_days_in_window) − committed_tonnage`, retrieve the most recent `ai_supply_forecast` record for the primary supplying cooperative for the same quarterly period and use the lower bound tonnage as the conservative raw material availability estimate, and compare available capacity and raw material estimate to the export order's required production tonnage. If available production capacity is less than required tonnage or raw material lower bound is less than required tonnage, the system shall create an `ai_export_conflict` record with export order ID, required tonnage, available production capacity, raw material lower bound, capacity gap value, conflict timestamp, and conflict type (`capacity` / `raw_material` / `both`), and display a non-blocking Conflict Alert on the order save screen: "Production feasibility conflict: [gap description]. The order has been saved. Recommended actions: [list of up to 3 options based on conflict type]." The Sales Manager shall be able to save the export order regardless of the conflict; the `ai_export_conflict` record shall remain linked to the order and visible in the Export Conflicts dashboard accessible to the Export Manager and Production Director roles.
