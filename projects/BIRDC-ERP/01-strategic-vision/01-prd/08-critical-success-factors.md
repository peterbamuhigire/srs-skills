# Section 7: Critical Success Factors

The following criteria define "done" for the BIRDC ERP project. Each criterion is deterministic: it has a clear pass/fail state and does not require subjective judgement.

## 7.1 Module Adoption

The system shall be accepted as complete only when all 17 modules are live and in daily operational use by the respective department users, within the agreed delivery roadmap timeline.

## 7.2 Financial Reporting Readiness

The Finance Director shall be able to generate a Trial Balance, Profit and Loss statement, Balance Sheet, and Budget vs. Actual report — for both parliamentary (PIBID) and commercial (BIRDC IFRS) modes — at any moment without closing an accounting period. These reports shall require zero manual data assembly from sources outside the system.

## 7.3 Agent Cash Balance Visibility

Every one of the 1,071 agents' real-time cash balances shall be visible in the system at all times. The cash balance for each agent shall equal the sum of all invoices issued to the agent's customers minus the sum of all verified remittances, computed automatically. Zero manual calculation shall be required.

## 7.4 Supply Chain Traceability

The Procurement Manager shall be able to trace every kilogramme of matooke from farm delivery (individual farmer name and NIN) through factory processing to finished product sale or by-product output — within the system, without reference to any external record.

## 7.5 OAG Audit Self-Sufficiency

The external auditor (OAG Uganda) shall be able to conduct a full annual audit using data and reports from the BIRDC ERP system alone. The audit shall require no manual reconciliation and no data reconstruction from paper records. The GL hash chain integrity report shall demonstrate tamper-evident records meeting Uganda Companies Act requirements.

## 7.6 PPDA Compliance

Every procurement transaction in the system shall carry a complete PPDA classification and the full set of required supporting documents. A PPDA procurement documentation compliance review shall find zero missing required documents on completed transactions.

## 7.7 Government Replication Readiness

The replication framework shall be documented in a separate technical guide. The system shall be demonstrable — by configuration change alone, with no code modification — to at least one additional Uganda government agro-processing entity within 6 months of go-live.
