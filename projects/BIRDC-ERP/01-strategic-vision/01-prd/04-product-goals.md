# Section 3: Product Goals

The following 5 strategic goals define the product's purpose. Every module, feature, and requirement in this document traces to at least one of these goals.

## Goal 1: Operational Unity

Replace fragmented spreadsheets and manual registers with one integrated system covering all 17 operational domains — from cooperative farmer delivery intake to export Certificate of Analysis issuance. The system shall provide a single source of truth for all operational data, eliminating reconciliation between disconnected tools.

*Success criterion:* All 17 modules live and in daily use within the delivery roadmap timeline.

## Goal 2: Financial Integrity

Provide a dual-mode accounting system that tracks PIBID parliamentary budget votes and BIRDC commercial accounts simultaneously. The system shall maintain an immutable, hash-chained General Ledger audit trail meeting Uganda Companies Act and Uganda Income Tax Act 7-year retention requirements, satisfying both OAG audit requirements and ICPAU/IFRS reporting standards.

*Success criterion:* The Finance Director can generate a Trial Balance, Profit and Loss statement, and Budget vs. Actual report for both parliamentary and commercial modes at any moment, without period closing. The external auditor requires no manual reconciliation.

## Goal 3: Agent Accountability

Eliminate the cash accountability gap in BIRDC's 1,071-strong field agent network. The system shall maintain a real-time agent cash balance (total sales proceeds minus verified remittances) for every agent at all times, with automated First In, First Out (FIFO) remittance allocation enforced by stored procedure.

*Success criterion:* Every one of the 1,071 agents' cash balances is visible in real time. Agent cash reconciliation requires zero manual calculation.

## Goal 4: Circular Economy Visibility

Provide the first ERP mass balance for Uganda's banana value chain: every kilogramme of input matooke accounted for across primary products (Tooke flour, chips, juice, fibre), by-products (biogas from peels, bio-slurry fertiliser from waste water), and scrap. The system shall prevent a production order from closing until the mass balance equation is satisfied within a configurable tolerance.

*Success criterion:* The Procurement Manager can trace every kilogramme of matooke from farm delivery through processing to finished product sale or by-product disposal. Mass balance reporting is available on demand.

## Goal 5: Government Replicability

Build every BIRDC-specific business rule in configuration tables — not in code — so the system can be redeployed for any Uganda government agro-processing entity without a codebase rebuild. PPDA thresholds, tax bands, commission rates, recipe ingredients, and cooperative structures shall all be administrator-configurable.

*Success criterion:* The replication framework is documented and demonstrated to at least one additional Uganda government entity. A qualified IT administrator can redeploy the system for a new entity by changing configuration only.
