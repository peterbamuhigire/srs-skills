# Section 2: Business Context

## 2.1 Organisational Profile

PIBID was established in 2005 by presidential directive to develop Uganda's banana value chain into an industrial enterprise. Over 20 years, the government invested approximately UGX 200 billion (~USD 54 million) to build the Nyaruzinga processing factory, develop the Tooke product range, and establish a nationwide cooperative farmer network. BIRDC is the commercially oriented successor entity, responsible for converting that investment into a self-sustaining, export-capable agro-processing business.

**Dual nature:** PIBID remains the statutory government body — its budget is voted by Uganda Parliament, its procurement must comply with the PPDA Act, and its accounts are subject to audit by the Office of the Auditor General (OAG) Uganda. BIRDC operates as the commercial arm — producing and selling Tooke branded products, entering export contracts, employing commercial staff, and required to produce IFRS-compliant financial statements.

The ERP system must serve both identities simultaneously, without requiring separate systems or manual data re-entry.

## 2.3 The Current Problem State

BIRDC currently operates with fragmented tools: manual spreadsheets for sales tracking, separate registers for agent cash balances, a non-integrated accounting package (if any), and paper-based cooperative farmer intake records. The consequences are:

- Agent cash balances cannot be determined in real time. Unreconciled cash gaps surface only at manual period-end review.
- Cooperative farmer payments are calculated manually from paper delivery records. Individual farmer contribution disputes cannot be resolved without reworking paper files.
- EFRIS submission is either absent or handled manually, creating URA compliance risk.
- PPDA procurement documentation is managed outside any integrated system, creating audit exposure.
- Parliamentary budget votes and commercial accounts cannot be produced from the same data set, requiring double-entry work.
- Production mass balance — tracking every kilogramme of matooke from intake through primary products, by-products, and scrap — is not measured.

## 2.4 The Business Opportunity

BIRDC holds signed or prospective export contracts with buyers in South Korea, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Italy, and the United States. Scaling export volumes requires Certificate of Analysis (CoA) documentation for every outbound batch — a laboratory quality management capability that must be built into the ERP production and QC workflow. No current system supports this.

The replication potential is equally significant. If BIRDC ERP is built with every BIRDC-specific rule in configuration tables (Design Covenant DC-007), the same codebase can be redeployed for Uganda Coffee Development Board, National Enterprise Corporation, or any Uganda government agro-processor — multiplying the public return on this investment.
