## 1. Delivery Model

### 1.1 Engagement Structure

This engagement uses a **fixed-price, milestone-based delivery model** with formal sign-off gates. Development proceeds phase by phase; no subsequent phase begins until the client formally accepts the preceding phase deliverable and the associated payment is released.

**Principal Consultant:** Peter Bamuhigire, ICT Consultant (techguypeter.com)

Peter Bamuhigire is personally responsible for:

- System architecture design and all technical decisions.
- Writing and maintaining the SRS, design documents, and test specifications.
- Recruiting, directing, and quality-gating all hired developers.
- Conducting all formal client demonstrations.
- Obtaining and filing all sign-off documents before payment is claimed.
- Ensuring the delivered system satisfies each phase gate criteria defined in Section 4 of this document.

**Hired Developers (1–3 as required per phase):**

Developers are contracted by Peter Bamuhigire to implement specific modules under his technical direction. Developers do not communicate directly with BIRDC/PIBID management on scope or requirements. All developer output is reviewed and approved by Peter Bamuhigire before it is demonstrated to the client.

### 1.2 Methodology

The project uses a **Hybrid delivery methodology**: each phase constitutes a formal milestone with:

- A fixed scope of modules and deliverables defined in advance.
- A formal client demonstration upon completion.
- A written sign-off from the Finance Director and the BIRDC Director.
- A payment trigger — no payment is released without written sign-off.
- A formal kickoff for the next phase after payment is confirmed.

Scope changes during a phase are handled via the Change Control procedure defined in Section 6. No requirement creep is absorbed without a written change request and Director sign-off.

### 1.3 Delivery Environment

Development and staging is hosted on BIRDC's own infrastructure at Nyaruzinga. Internet connectivity at Nyaruzinga is intermittent; the development environment is therefore configured to operate fully offline where possible. All BIRDC data remains on BIRDC infrastructure at all times (Design Constraint DC-006).
