## 5. Change Control

### 5.1 Scope Freeze

The scope of each milestone is fixed at kickoff. No new requirement may be added to an in-progress milestone without following this procedure. Requirements already documented in the SRS are in scope. Undocumented requirements are out of scope until a Change Request is raised and approved.

### 5.2 Change Request Procedure

1. The requesting party (BIRDC/PIBID staff or Peter Bamuhigire) submits a written **Change Request (CR)** form identifying the requested change, the reason, and the affected milestone.
2. Peter Bamuhigire performs an **impact assessment** within 5 business days: scope impact, effort estimate (developer-days), cost impact (if applicable), and effect on the milestone delivery date.
3. The impact assessment is presented to the Finance Director and the BIRDC Director.
4. If the change increases scope or cost, the Director signs off on a **Change Authorisation** document before implementation begins. The fixed price for the affected milestone is amended accordingly.
5. If the change is within the agreed scope (a clarification rather than a new feature), Peter Bamuhigire implements it with no fee adjustment and records it in the project log.
6. Changes that cannot be absorbed into the current milestone without delaying sign-off are deferred to the next milestone and documented in the project log.

### 5.3 No Verbal Change Orders

No scope change is valid unless documented in a signed Change Authorisation. Verbal instructions from any BIRDC staff member, including senior management, do not constitute an authorised change order.
