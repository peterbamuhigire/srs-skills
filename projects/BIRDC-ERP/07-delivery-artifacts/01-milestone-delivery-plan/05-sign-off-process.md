## 4. Formal Sign-Off Process

### 4.1 Demonstration

Upon completing all deliverables within a milestone, Peter Bamuhigire schedules a formal demonstration with the Finance Director (STK-002) and the BIRDC Director (STK-001). The demonstration covers:

1. Live walkthrough of every module delivered in the milestone.
2. Execution of all phase gate criteria listed in Section 3.
3. Verification of all performance thresholds specified in the Phase Gate Criteria & Metrics document.
4. Review of any open GAP items affecting the milestone and confirmation of resolution or documented deferral.

The demonstration is conducted on the staging environment hosted on BIRDC's own infrastructure.

### 4.2 Sign-Off Document

Following a successful demonstration, Peter Bamuhigire issues a **Milestone Acceptance Certificate** to the Finance Director and the BIRDC Director. The certificate states:

- The milestone number and name.
- The list of modules and applications delivered.
- A declaration that all phase gate criteria have been satisfied.
- Any items formally deferred to a later milestone (with Director agreement).
- Signature lines for the Finance Director and the BIRDC Director.
- The date of acceptance.

Both signatories must sign the certificate before any payment is raised.

### 4.3 Payment Release

Upon receipt of the signed Milestone Acceptance Certificate, Peter Bamuhigire raises a payment invoice against the agreed fixed-price for that milestone. Payment is due within the terms stated in the engagement agreement. The next milestone kickoff is scheduled only after payment is confirmed.

### 4.4 Next Milestone Kickoff

A formal kickoff meeting for the subsequent milestone is held within 5 business days of payment confirmation. The kickoff establishes:

- Confirmed scope for the next milestone (no changes to scope without following the Change Control procedure).
- Developer assignments and availability.
- Resolution plan for any open GAP items affecting the next milestone.
- Target demonstration date.
