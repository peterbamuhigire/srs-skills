# Section 9: The Director — Executive Dashboard

The Director uses the Executive Dashboard App on his Android phone. The app gives a real-time view of the most important numbers without requiring any navigation through menus.

---

## 9.1 Viewing the Executive Dashboard App

1. Open the **Executive Dashboard App** on your phone. It opens directly to the dashboard — no menu navigation needed.
2. The dashboard shows the following panels:
   - **Today's Revenue** — total sales invoiced today (BIRDC commercial)
   - **Cash Position** — total cash and mobile money in bank accounts as of the latest bank sync
   - **Agent Balances** — total outstanding cash across all 1,071 agents
   - **Production Today** — kilograms produced today vs. target
   - **Budget vs. Actual** — current month's expenditure against budget for each parliamentary vote
3. Tap any panel to drill down into the detail.
4. Pull down on the screen to refresh all numbers.

---

## 9.2 Reading the Daily Sales Push Notification

The system sends you a push notification every day at a time configured by the Finance Director (default: 08:00 AM).

1. When you see the notification on your phone screen, tap it to open it.
2. The notification shows:
   - Yesterday's total sales (UGX)
   - Number of invoices issued
   - Top-selling product
   - Number of agents with balances over their 30-day average (a flag for review)
3. Tap **Open Dashboard** within the notification to go directly to the Executive Dashboard App for more detail.

*If you stop receiving daily notifications, contact the IT Administrator to check the notification settings.*

---

## 9.3 Checking Budget vs. Actual for a Parliamentary Vote

1. Open the Executive Dashboard App.
2. Tap the **Budget vs. Actual** panel.
3. A list of all PIBID parliamentary votes appears (for example, Development Vote, Recurrent Vote).
4. Each vote shows:
   - **Approved Budget (UGX)** for the financial year
   - **Spent to Date (UGX)**
   - **Remaining (UGX)**
   - **Percentage Used** — shown as a colour-coded bar (green = below 80%, orange = 80–95%, red = above 95%)
5. Tap any vote to see a breakdown of expenditure by category.
6. Votes shown in orange have reached 80% of their allocation — an automatic alert has already been sent to the Finance Director.
7. Votes shown in red have reached 95% of their allocation — you have also received an alert.

*Spending that would exceed 100% of a vote requires your personal approval with a written justification. The Finance Director will contact you if this situation arises.*
