# Topic 5: Farmers and Procurement

---

**Q25. What happens if a farmer is not registered when their matooke arrives at the factory gate?**

The Collections Officer must register the farmer on the spot using the Farmer Delivery App before recording their delivery. The app supports offline farmer registration — no internet is needed (see the User Manual, Section 8.1). A delivery cannot be assigned to an unregistered farmer. This rule (BR-011) exists because every kilogramme of matooke in a batch must be traceable to a specific registered farmer before the batch can advance to stock receipt. If the farmer refuses to provide their National Identification Number (NIN), the Collections Officer should escalate to their supervisor — NIN is mandatory for farmer registration.

**Q26. Can one farmer belong to more than one cooperative?**

No. A farmer's record is linked to exactly one cooperative at a time. BIRDC's procurement structure organises farmers into cooperatives, and cooperative batch records track which cooperative delivered which batch. If a farmer moves from one cooperative to another, the Procurement Manager updates the farmer's cooperative assignment in the system. Historical delivery records remain linked to the cooperative the farmer was registered with at the time of each delivery.

**Q27. How are farmer deductions (input loans) applied to payments?**

When a farmer has an outstanding input loan or cooperative levy, the system deducts the scheduled repayment amount from the net payable for each batch delivery. The deduction is calculated automatically when the farmer payment schedule is generated (step 4 in the 5-stage cooperative procurement workflow). The farmer's payment record shows gross payable, each deduction with its description, and net payable. The farmer receives an SMS notification when their payment is processed, including the net amount paid and the deductions applied.

**Q28. What PPDA documents are required for a small procurement?**

For a small procurement (value above the micro threshold but at or below the small procurement ceiling), the following documents are required:

1. Approved Purchase Request.
2. Request for Quotations (RFQ) sent to at least 3 suppliers.
3. Quotation Evaluation Report comparing the responses.
4. Local Purchase Order (LPO) issued to the selected supplier.
5. Goods Receipt Note (GRN) confirming the goods were received.
6. Supplier Invoice.
7. Finance Manager approval record and Procurement Officer sign-off.

The BIRDC ERP tracks all these documents within the procurement record. A payment cannot be processed if any required document is missing — the payment screen will show a warning listing the missing items.

---

**Q29. How do I find the current PPDA procurement thresholds?**

The current thresholds are configured in the system by the IT Administrator. To view them:

1. Click **Procurement** then **PPDA Settings**.
2. The thresholds for micro, small, large, and restricted procurement are shown.

If you believe a threshold needs to be updated (for example, following a PPDA regulation change), ask the IT Administrator to update it. The IT Administrator requires written instruction from the Procurement Manager or Finance Director to change a PPDA threshold.
