# Topic 2: Agent and Remittance

---

**Q8. Why is my agent cash balance different from what I calculated manually?**

The most common reason is that one or more of your remittances are submitted but not yet verified by a supervisor. A remittance you submitted only reduces your cash balance after a supervisor confirms it. Check the **Remittance History** screen in the Sales Agent App — look for remittances with a status of "Pending Verification." Once a supervisor verifies them, your balance will update. If the discrepancy remains after all your remittances are verified, contact the Finance Manager with a screenshot of your balance screen and your manual calculation for reconciliation.

---

**Q9. Why was my remittance allocated to an old invoice I thought was already cleared?**

The system allocates remittances using a strict rule called FIFO — First In, First Out. This means your remittance is always applied to the oldest outstanding invoice first, regardless of which invoice you think it should clear. If you believed an invoice was cleared but still see a balance, it is likely that an earlier invoice had a remaining unpaid amount that was cleared first. Open the **Remittance History** screen and tap on the remittance to see the exact allocation breakdown. If you believe there is an error, contact the Finance Manager with the remittance reference number.

**Q10. How do I increase an agent's stock float limit?**

Only the Sales Manager can change an agent's float limit. The Sales Manager should:

1. Log in to the web application.
2. Click **Sales** then **Agent Management**.
3. Search for and open the agent's record.
4. Click **Edit** and update the **Stock Float Limit** field.
5. Click **Save**.

The new limit takes effect immediately. Stock issuance that was previously blocked will be allowed once the limit is increased.

---

**Q11. What happens when an agent is suspended?**

When the Sales Manager suspends an agent's account:

- The agent cannot log in to the Sales Agent App.
- No new stock can be issued to the agent.
- Existing sales already recorded on the agent's phone will still sync when connectivity is available.
- The agent's outstanding cash balance remains on record and must be settled before the account can be reactivated.

To reactivate a suspended agent, the Sales Manager opens the agent's record and changes the status from **Suspended** to **Active**. This requires Finance Manager co-approval if the agent's outstanding balance exceeds their configured credit threshold.

---

**Q12. Why does my commission statement show zero for some months?**

Commission only accrues on verified sales. If your remittances for that month were submitted but not verified by a supervisor, no commission is generated. Check the remittance status for that month in the **Remittance History** screen. Remittances marked "Pending Verification" have not generated commission. Once a supervisor verifies them, the commission will appear on your next statement. Commission is never backdated manually — it is calculated automatically when verification happens, regardless of when that verification takes place.

---

**Q13. Can an agent see another agent's cash balance?**

No. Each agent can only see their own cash balance, stock balance, and commission statement. The Sales Manager and Finance Manager can see all agents' balances across the full network.
