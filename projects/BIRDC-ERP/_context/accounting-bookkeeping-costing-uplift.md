# BIRDC-ERP Accounting and Costing Uplift

## Purpose

This note applies bookkeeping, ERP finance, inventory accounting, and management cost accounting guidance to BIRDC-ERP requirements and design work.

## Product Implications

- Link farmer purchases, receiving, grading, production batches, inventory movements, processing outputs, quality losses, export sales, grants, assets, and payments to auditable finance events.
- Use cost centres for factory, research, procurement, farmer engagement, quality, administration, stores, and export logistics where reporting requires accountability.
- Support batch/product costing: raw banana input, additives/packaging, labour, utilities, overhead, yield, scrap, rework, quality rejection, finished goods, and by-product recovery where relevant.
- Reconcile inventory valuation to GL by raw materials, WIP, finished goods, packaging, spares, damaged/expired stock, and in-transit stock.
- Track grant or public-funding spend by fund/project/donor where the implementation requires restricted-fund accountability.
- Add finance close evidence for bank, mobile money, suppliers, farmer payables, inventory, fixed assets, tax, payroll, grants, and suspense accounts.

## Requirement Prompt

Any BIRDC operational workflow that moves material, creates payable obligations, consumes resources, or generates sales should identify its accounting entry, cost object, inventory valuation effect, source evidence, approval path, and reconciliation point.
