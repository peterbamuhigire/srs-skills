# Education: Regulations & Standards Reference

## FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act)

| Provision | Citation | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Right to Inspect | 20 U.S.C. §1232g(a)(1) | Parents/eligible students may inspect and review education records |
| Right to Amend | 20 U.S.C. §1232g(a)(2) | Right to request correction of inaccurate records |
| Consent for Disclosure | 20 U.S.C. §1232g(b)(1) | Written consent required before disclosing education records to third parties |
| Directory Information | 34 CFR §99.37 | Schools may disclose directory info without consent unless parent/student opts out |
| School Officials Exception | 34 CFR §99.31(a)(1) | Disclosure to school officials with legitimate educational interest permitted without consent |

### FERPA Key Definitions

- **Education Records:** Any records directly related to a student maintained by the educational agency
- **Personally Identifiable Information (PII):** Name, address, SSN, student ID, or other direct/indirect identifiers
- **Eligible Student:** Student aged 18 or attending a postsecondary institution (rights transfer from parent)

## COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act)

- Applies to online services directed to children under 13 or with actual knowledge of users under 13
- **Verifiable Parental Consent (VPC):** Required before collecting, using, or disclosing personal information from children under 13
- **Safe Harbor Provisions:** Schools may provide consent on behalf of parents for educational purposes (school-issued accounts)
- Data collected from children under 13 must not be used for targeted advertising
- Operators must maintain a privacy policy describing data collection and sharing practices

## WCAG 2.1 Level AA (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines)

| Principle | Key Requirements |
|---|---|
| Perceivable | Alt text for images, captions for video/audio, sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 minimum) |
| Operable | All functionality available via keyboard, skip navigation links, no seizure-inducing content |
| Understandable | Language of page identified, error messages descriptive and actionable |
| Robust | HTML validates, ARIA landmarks used correctly, compatible with screen readers |

## Section 508 (Rehabilitation Act)

- Federal agencies and recipients of federal funding must ensure ICT is accessible to people with disabilities
- Applies to any educational institution receiving federal funding (Title IV programs)
- Technical standards incorporate WCAG 2.0 Level AA by reference (36 CFR Part 1194)
- Covers web, software, electronic documents, and multimedia

## CIPA (Children's Internet Protection Act)

- Schools and libraries receiving E-Rate funding must implement internet safety policies
- Technology protection measures (filtering) required for visual depictions of obscenity and child pornography
- Monitors online activities of minors as part of the internet safety policy
- Applies to all computers with internet access at the institution
