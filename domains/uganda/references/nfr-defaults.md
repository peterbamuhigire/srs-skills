## Data Protection and Privacy (DPPA 2019)

**NFR-DPPA-001 — Personal Data Classification**
The system shall classify all stored data fields as: (a) non-personal, (b) personal, or (c) special personal data (financial information, health status, medical records, religious/philosophical beliefs, political opinions, sexual life) per Section 2 of the DPPA 2019.

**NFR-DPPA-002 — Special Personal Data Encryption**
All special personal data fields — including financial information (mobile money numbers, salary amounts, bank account details, payment histories) — shall be encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM. Personal data fields shall be encrypted at rest using a minimum of AES-128.

**NFR-DPPA-003 — Consent Prior to Collection**
The system shall not collect or process personal data without recording the data subject's prior, freely-given, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent (Section 7, DPPA 2019), unless a Section 7(2) lawful alternative basis applies and is documented.

**NFR-DPPA-004 — Data Subject Rights — 30-Day Response**
The system shall support data subject requests for access, rectification, and erasure. Every request shall be logged with a timestamp. The system shall surface overdue requests (exceeding 30 calendar days) to the DPO dashboard. Written rejection with reasons shall be generated by the system where compliance is not possible (Section 16(3), DPPA 2019).

**NFR-DPPA-005 — Immediate Breach Notification Workflow**
Upon detection of unauthorised access or acquisition of personal data, the system shall: (a) halt further exposure immediately, (b) generate a PDPO notification report containing: nature of breach, data involved, categories and approximate number of affected data subjects, likely consequences, and remedial measures taken, (c) record the notification timestamp. The notification report shall be generated and surfaced to the DPO within 1 hour of detection (Section 23, DPPA 2019; Regulation 33).

**NFR-DPPA-006 — Retention and Destruction Schedule**
The system shall enforce a configurable retention period per data category. At expiry, personal data shall be: (a) de-identified (preferred for statistical/historical retention), or (b) destroyed in a manner that prevents reconstruction in intelligible form (Section 18(4)-(5), DPPA 2019).

**NFR-DPPA-007 — PDPO Registration Record**
The system shall store the organisation's PDPO registration number and registration date in the administration module. A configuration warning shall be displayed until a registration number is entered.

**NFR-DPPA-008 — DPO Contact Record**
The system shall store and display the designated Data Protection Officer's name, role, and contact details in the administration module (Section 6, DPPA 2019; Regulation 47).

**NFR-DPPA-009 — Children's Data Safeguard**
Where the system collects data that may relate to individuals under 18 years, it shall enforce collection of parent/guardian consent and log the consent relationship (Section 8, DPPA 2019; Regulation 11).

**NFR-DPPA-010 — Cross-border Transfer Restriction**
The system shall not transmit or store personal data on servers outside Uganda unless: (a) a configuration record confirms the destination country has adequate protection equivalent to the DPPA 2019, or (b) explicit data subject consent is recorded. ## OAG Audit Trail and Retention (Uganda Income Tax Act + Companies Act)

**NFR-OAG-001 — 7-Year Retention**
All financial records (GL entries, journals, invoices, receipts, payroll runs, procurement documents) shall be retained for a minimum of 7 years from the date of the financial transaction, as required by the Uganda Income Tax Act (Cap 340) and the Uganda Companies Act.

**NFR-OAG-002 — Tamper-Evident Audit Trail**
Every GL entry shall include: entry timestamp (UTC), user ID, IP address, document reference number, debit account, credit amount, and a cryptographic integrity mechanism (e.g., SHA-256 hash chain per BR-013) to detect post-entry modification. Any detected tampering shall be flagged on the system health dashboard and reported to the Finance Director.

**NFR-OAG-003 — OAG Export**
The system shall export the trial balance, GL detail ledger, and full audit trail for any user-specified period in Excel (.xlsx) and CSV formats, suitable for submission to OAG review teams.

**NFR-OAG-004 — Audit Trail Query Performance**
Any audit trail query for a 30-day period for any user shall return results in ≤ 5 seconds at P95 under normal load.

## PPDA Procurement Compliance (PPDA Act Cap 305)

**NFR-PPDA-001 — Procurement Document Checklist**
The system shall enforce a category-specific PPDA documentation checklist for every purchase. Payment release shall be blocked at the API layer until all required documents are filed and approved. A missing document shall return HTTP 422 with error code `ERR_PPDA_DOCS_INCOMPLETE`.

**NFR-PPDA-002 — Procurement Register**
The system shall maintain a PPDA procurement register exportable to Excel, showing all purchases with: LPO number, vendor, category, threshold level, approval authority, document status, and payment status.

**NFR-PPDA-003 — Threshold Configuration**
PPDA procurement approval thresholds shall be configurable by the System Administrator (not hardcoded) to accommodate regulatory updates. [CONTEXT-GAP: GAP-007 — confirm current PPDA threshold UGX values applicable to this entity.]

## URA EFRIS Integration (Uganda Revenue Authority)

**NFR-EFRIS-001 — Real-Time Submission**
Every commercial invoice and POS receipt shall be submitted to URA EFRIS via the system-to-system API immediately on document posting (asynchronously — must not block user workflow).

**NFR-EFRIS-002 — FDN Storage and Printing**
The Fiscal Document Number (FDN) returned by EFRIS shall be stored in the database linked to the originating document and printed on all physical and digital copies of that document.

**NFR-EFRIS-003 — Retry Queue**
Failed EFRIS submissions shall be placed in a retry queue. Retry shall use exponential backoff (initial delay 30 s, multiplier 2, maximum 10 retries). The EFRIS retry queue length and failure rate shall be displayed on the system administration dashboard.

## PAYE and NSSF Payroll Compliance (Uganda Income Tax Act + NSSF Act)

**NFR-PAY-001 — Configurable PAYE Bands**
PAYE tax bands and rates shall be stored as configurable data (not hardcoded) to permit annual updates without code changes. [CONTEXT-GAP: GAP-008 — confirm current URA PAYE bands for 2024/25 tax year.]

**NFR-PAY-002 — NSSF Schedule Format**
The system shall generate the monthly NSSF employer remittance schedule in the format prescribed by NSSF Uganda. [CONTEXT-GAP: GAP-009 — confirm exact NSSF schedule format required.]

**NFR-PAY-003 — 7-Year Payroll Retention**
All payroll run records shall be retained for 7 years per Uganda Income Tax Act requirements. Payroll records shall be immutable after Finance Manager lock (no deletion, no amendment — correction runs only).

## Farmer and Agricultural Data Privacy (DPPA 2019 — Special Application)

**NFR-FAR-001 — Farmer PII Encryption**
Farmer personal data — specifically NIN, GPS farm coordinates, photograph, and mobile money number — shall be encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM. Mobile money number is **special personal data** (financial information) under Section 9 DPPA 2019.

**NFR-FAR-002 — Farmer Consent Record**
The system shall record explicit consent from each farmer (or their cooperative representative on their behalf) before collecting NIN, GPS coordinates, photograph, and mobile money number. Consent records shall be stored with timestamp and collector ID.

**NFR-FAR-003 — Farmer Data Access Restriction**
Access to farmer PII shall be restricted to Procurement, Finance, and System Administrator roles. Access attempts by other roles shall return HTTP 403.

**NFR-FAR-004 — DPIA Trigger**
The collection of GPS coordinates, NIN, photographs, and mobile money numbers from 6,000+ farmers constitutes large-scale processing of personal data and special personal data. A DPIA shall be completed and documented before Phase 3 (farmer registration) goes live. [CONTEXT-GAP: GAP-004 — DPPA 2019 legal review required before go-live.]
