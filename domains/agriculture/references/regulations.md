# Agriculture: Regulations & Standards Reference

## Uganda Data Protection and Privacy Act 2019

| Provision | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Consent for GPS Collection | Farmers must give informed consent before the system collects or stores GPS coordinates of their land |
| Data Retention | Personal data shall not be retained longer than is necessary for the purpose for which it was collected; retention periods must be disclosed |
| Third-Party Sharing for Traceability | Sharing farmer data with buyers, exporters, or certification bodies requires explicit consent specifying the recipient and purpose |
| Farmer Consent for Data Use | Any use of farmer data beyond the stated purpose (e.g., analytics, aggregated reporting) requires separate consent |

## EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) 2023/1115

| Provision | Requirement |
|---|---|
| GPS Polygon Requirements | Each production plot must be geolocated with GPS polygon boundaries (≤ 4 ha plots: single point with area; > 4 ha plots: full polygon) |
| Deforestation Baseline | Land must not have been subject to deforestation after 31 December 2020 |
| Due Diligence Statement (DDS) | Operators must submit a DDS before placing commodities on the EU market, declaring deforestation-free status |
| Covered Commodities | Coffee, cocoa, timber, soy, palm oil, rubber, cattle — and derived products |
| EU TRACES Submission | DDS must be submitted through the EU Trade Control and Expert System (TRACES) information system |

### Key EUDR Technical Requirements

- **Geolocation Accuracy:** GPS coordinates accurate to within ±10 metres
- **Polygon Format:** GeoJSON or equivalent machine-readable format
- **Baseline Verification:** Satellite imagery comparison against December 2020 forest cover data
- **Supply Chain Mapping:** Full traceability from production plot to point of export

## GlobalGAP

- **Standard:** Integrated Farm Assurance (IFA) v6
- **Use:** Certification for Good Agricultural Practices required by EU and UK supermarket buyers
- **Scope:** Crop protection product usage, worker health and safety, environmental management, traceability
- **Audit Cycle:** Annual on-site audit with unannounced inspections
- **Reference:** https://www.globalgap.org/

## Rainforest Alliance / UTZ (2020 Merged Standard)

- **Standard:** Rainforest Alliance 2020 Certification Programme
- **Commodities:** Coffee, tea, cocoa
- **Key Requirements:** Sustainability differential payments, farm-level risk assessments, traceability from first point of purchase, child labour remediation
- **Audit Cycle:** 3-year certification cycle with annual surveillance audits
- **Reference:** https://www.rainforest-alliance.org/

## Uganda Employment Act 2006

| Provision | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Minimum Wage | No statutory minimum wage; contracts must specify agreed wage |
| Working Hours | Maximum 48 hours per week; overtime at 1.5x normal rate |
| Annual Leave | 7 working days per 4 continuous months of service |
| Sick Leave | 1 month on full pay, 1 month on half pay per 12-month period |
| Termination | Written notice required; notice period depends on pay frequency (2 weeks for monthly employees) |
| Child Labour | Minimum employment age 16 years; hazardous work prohibited under 18 years |

## National Social Security Fund (NSSF) Act

- **Employer Contribution:** 10% of employee's gross monthly wage
- **Employee Contribution:** 5% of gross monthly wage (deducted at source)
- **Applicability:** Mandatory for employers with 5 or more employees; voluntary for smaller farms
- **Remittance:** Monthly, by the 15th of the following month
- **Registration:** Both employer and employee must be registered with NSSF

## PAYE (Pay As You Earn)

- **Legislation:** Uganda Income Tax Act, Cap 340
- **Applicability:** All employees earning above the tax-free threshold (UGX 235,000/month)
- **Rates:** Progressive tax bands as gazetted by Uganda Revenue Authority
- **Remittance:** Employer withholds and remits to URA by the 15th of the following month

## ISO 22000: Food Safety Management Systems

- **Standard:** ISO 22000:2018
- **Scope:** Requirements for any organisation in the food chain, including farm-level processing and post-harvest handling
- **Key Elements:** Hazard analysis, critical control points (HACCP), prerequisite programmes, traceability, management review
- **Relevance:** Required for farms with on-site processing or direct export operations

## Codex Alimentarius

| Area | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Pesticide Residue Limits | Maximum Residue Limits (MRLs) per crop-pesticide combination as listed in Codex MRL database |
| Quality Grading | Commodity-specific grading standards (e.g., green coffee defect count, cocoa bean fermentation percentage) |
| Food Hygiene | General principles of food hygiene (CXC 1-1969, Rev. 2020) |
| Labelling | Pre-packaged food labelling standards for export (CXS 1-1985) |

## UCDA (Uganda Coffee Development Authority) Regulations

- **Farmer Registration:** All coffee farmers must be registered in the UCDA national coffee database
- **Export Licensing:** Coffee exporters must hold a valid UCDA export licence
- **Quality Standards:** Export coffee must meet UCDA quality grading (screen size, moisture content ≤ 12.5%, defect count)
- **Traceability:** Coffee must be traceable to parish level at minimum

## NAADS / Operation Wealth Creation (OWC) Programme Rules

- **Input Distribution:** Government-subsidised seeds, seedlings, and livestock distributed through OWC
- **Eligibility:** Registered farmer groups and individual farmers within targeted sub-counties
- **Reporting:** Beneficiary tracking, input utilisation reports, and yield data collection required
- **Compliance:** Systems handling OWC data must support government reporting formats

## Uganda Wildlife Act 2019

- **Relevance:** Farms adjacent to or within buffer zones of protected areas (national parks, wildlife reserves)
- **Restrictions:** Prohibition on encroachment; buffer zone farming practices regulated by Uganda Wildlife Authority
- **Human-Wildlife Conflict:** Reporting requirements for crop damage by wildlife; compensation claims process
- **Environmental Impact:** Farms converting natural habitat must comply with Environmental Impact Assessment requirements under NEMA
