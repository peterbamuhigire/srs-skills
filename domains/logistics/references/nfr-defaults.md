# Logistics: Default Non-Functional Requirements

These requirements are auto-injected into new logistics project scaffolds.
All blocks are tagged `[DOMAIN-DEFAULT: logistics]` for consultant review.

#### LOG-NFR-001: Real-Time Tracking Update Frequency
The system shall ingest, process, and reflect GPS position updates for all
active shipments within 5 minutes of the update being generated by the
telematics device, under normal network conditions.

**Verifiability:** Instrument a test vehicle with a GPS telematics device
configured to transmit at 1-minute intervals. Over a 60-minute test period,
record the delta between each device timestamp and the timestamp at which
the position is queryable via the tracking API. All 60 samples must show a
delta of $\leq 5$ minutes. Repeat under simulated high-load conditions
(1,000 concurrent active vehicles).

#### LOG-NFR-002: ETA Accuracy for Last-Mile Delivery
The system shall provide ETA estimates for last-mile delivery that are accurate
within 15 minutes of the actual delivery time, measured at the point the
shipment enters the final delivery zone (last hub departure scan).

**Verifiability:** Collect ETA predictions and actual delivery timestamps for
a sample of 500 last-mile deliveries across diverse routes. Calculate:
$ETA\_Error = |T_{predicted} - T_{actual}|$. The median error must be
$\leq 15$ minutes, and 90% of predictions must fall within a 30-minute window.
<!-- [END DOMAIN-DEFAULT] -->

#### LOG-NFR-003: System Availability for Shipment Processing
The system shall maintain 99.9% uptime availability ($\leq 8.76$ hours downtime
per year) for all shipment booking, tracking, and dispatch modules, measured
on a rolling 30-day basis. Planned maintenance windows must be scheduled
outside peak operating hours (06:00–22:00 local dispatch time).

**Verifiability:** Monitor uptime continuously using an independent monitoring
service. Calculate availability as:
$Availability = \frac{MTTF}{MTTF + MTTR} \times 100\%$
The result must be $\geq 99.9\%$ over the measurement window. Verify that no
planned maintenance occurs during designated peak operating hours without
prior stakeholder approval.
<!-- [END DOMAIN-DEFAULT] -->

#### LOG-NFR-004: Data Retention for Shipping Records
The system shall retain all shipment records, customs declarations, chain of
custody logs, and driver hours-of-service logs for a minimum of 7 years from
the shipment completion date, in compliance with U.S. Customs and Border
Protection record-keeping requirements (19 CFR Part 163) and DOT FMCSA
regulations (49 CFR §395.8(k)).

**Verifiability:** Attempt to permanently delete a shipment record with a
completion date fewer than 7 years ago. The system shall reject the deletion,
return an appropriate error message citing the retention policy, and log
the attempted deletion with the requesting user's identity. Verify via
database query that the record remains intact.
<!-- [END DOMAIN-DEFAULT] -->

#### LOG-NFR-005: Dangerous Goods Compliance Pre-Booking Validation
The system shall execute a mandatory dangerous goods compliance check before
confirming any shipment booking that includes items classified as dangerous
goods under IATA DGR, 49 CFR (DOT hazmat), or IMDG regulations. A booking
confirmation shall not be issued if the compliance check returns a violation.

**Verifiability:** Attempt to book a shipment containing a Class 9 lithium
battery exceeding the permitted watt-hour threshold for passenger aircraft
without providing the required Shipper's Declaration for Dangerous Goods.
The system shall reject the booking, return a specific error message identifying
the violated regulation and the specific limit exceeded, and log the rejection
with the shipment details and initiating user ID.
