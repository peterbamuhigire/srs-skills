# Logistics: Regulations & Standards Reference

## DOT FMCSA (Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration)

| Regulation | Citation | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Hours of Service (HOS) | 49 CFR Part 395 | Limits driving hours; requires 30-min breaks; 11-hour driving limit per shift |
| Electronic Logging Device (ELD) | 49 CFR Part 395.8 | ELD mandatory for most CMV drivers; must capture HOS data automatically |
| Vehicle Inspection | 49 CFR Part 396 | Pre-trip and post-trip inspections required; defects must be documented and corrected |
| Driver Qualification | 49 CFR Part 391 | CDL requirements, medical certification, driving record checks |
| Hazardous Materials | 49 CFR Parts 171–180 | Placarding, packaging, documentation, and driver training for hazmat shipments |

### FMCSA Electronic Logging Device (ELD) Requirements

- ELD must be tamper-resistant and certified to FMCSA technical specifications
- HOS data must be transferable to enforcement officers via telematics or manual display
- Driver must be identified on the ELD; co-driver team driving must be supported

## ISO 28000 (Supply Chain Security Management)

- Framework for security management systems in supply chain operations
- Requires risk assessment, security planning, and incident response for supply chain threats
- Covers physical security, personnel security, information security, and transportation security
- Applicable to freight forwarders, carriers, warehouses, and port operators

## IATA DGR (Dangerous Goods Regulations)

| Classification | Examples | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Class 1 | Explosives | Prohibited on most passenger aircraft; restricted cargo only |
| Class 3 | Flammable liquids | Quantity and packaging limits per IATA Table 3.1 |
| Class 8 | Corrosives | Separate storage from incompatible goods |
| Class 9 | Miscellaneous | Lithium batteries — strict watt-hour and packaging requirements |

- Shipper's Declaration for Dangerous Goods required for all DG shipments by air
- DG acceptance checklist must be completed by the carrier before booking acceptance
- Incompatible goods must not be co-loaded per IATA Segregation Table

## C-TPAT (Customs-Trade Partnership Against Terrorism)

- Voluntary U.S. CBP program requiring supply chain security standards for importers, carriers, and brokers
- Benefits: reduced border inspection frequency, priority processing, access to FAST lane
- Requirements: written security profile, risk assessment, business partner vetting, seal integrity procedures
- Annual self-assessment and periodic CBP validation visits

## Incoterms 2020 (International Chamber of Commerce)

| Term | Risk Transfer | Freight Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| EXW (Ex Works) | At seller's premises | Buyer bears all freight |
| FOB (Free on Board) | At named port, loaded on vessel | Split: seller to port, buyer from port |
| CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight) | At destination port | Seller pays freight and insurance |
| DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) | At buyer's premises | Seller bears all costs and risk |

- Incoterms must be explicitly stated on commercial invoices and waybills
- The applicable Incoterms edition (2020) must be referenced to avoid ambiguity
